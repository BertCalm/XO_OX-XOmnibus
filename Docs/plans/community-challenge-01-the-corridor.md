# Community Challenge #1 — The Corridor

**Launch date:** March 30, 2026
**Results announced:** April 6-12, 2026 (Week 3)
**Prize:** Winning preset added to the factory library (next release)

---

## The Challenge

Build a coupling patch where one engine drives the sonic evolution of another.

No multi-engine complexity required. Two engines. One route. Make the seam between them audible.

The challenge is named "The Corridor" because a corridor isn't a room — it's the space between rooms. A corridor has its own character: its length, its light, its resonance. You hear it differently when you're inside it versus looking from either end. The coupling route is the corridor. We want to hear the space between your two engines.

---

## Rules

**Engine count:** 2 engines loaded (Slots 1 and 2). Slots 3 and 4 must be empty.

**Coupling route:** At least 1 active coupling route. Type is your choice — all 14 are legal.

**Performance requirement:** The preset must respond to player input in a way that the coupling makes audible. Playing softly must produce a different sonic event than playing hard — not just quieter, but fundamentally different, because the coupling route is active.

**Submission format:** `.xometa` file + a short paragraph (2-5 sentences) describing what your coupling route does and why you chose the amount you used.

**One submission per person.** You can update your submission until the deadline.

---

## What We're Judging

**The seam.** Can you hear the corridor? Is the connection between the two engines audible as a sonic event, not just as background color?

**Intention.** Did you choose the coupling amount deliberately? Too much coupling makes both engines lose their identity. Too little makes the connection subliminal. The sweet spot is where the interaction is audible but both engines are still themselves.

**Surprise.** Does your combination produce a sound that neither engine could produce alone? If the patch sounds like two engines mixed together, the coupling isn't doing its job. If it sounds like a sound that doesn't have a category, you're in the right place.

🎯 **Community Challenge #1 — The Corridor**

Two engines. One coupling route. Make the seam between them audible.

The coupling system in XOceanus is 14 routing types that let one engine drive another's filter, pitch, morph, FM input, or physical parameters in real time. The Field Guide post on this drops this week (xo-ox.org/field-guide). But reading about it is different from designing with it.

**Your challenge:**

Load any 2 engines. Wire them together with any coupling type. The patch needs to do something in response to how you play it — not because of an LFO, not because of an envelope inside a single engine, but because the coupling route is carrying information between two separate synthesis architectures.

**Rules:**
- 2 engines only (slots 3 and 4 empty)
- 1+ active coupling route
- Soft playing vs. hard playing must sound meaningfully different
- Share your `.xometa` file + 2-5 sentences about your routing choices

**Prize:** The winning patch gets added to the factory library in the next release, credited to you in the preset description.

This challenge runs for two weeks. Drop your entries in #challenge-the-corridor.

Some starting points if you're stuck: the Field Guide post covers all 15 coupling types with specific parameter recommendations. The coupling amount sweet spot is almost always 0.08–0.20. The most musical coupling patches are usually the most restrained ones.
