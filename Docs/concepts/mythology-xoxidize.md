The iron sclerites on the foot serve as armor — the corrosion IS the protection. In OXIDIZE, the degraded sound IS the identity. The engine is most itself when the note has aged fully. The corrosion is not damage. It is becoming.

## MYTHOLOGY NARRATIVE

### The Snail That Armored Itself in Rust

At the boundary where superheated water meets the cold abyss, minerals precipitate from solution. Iron sulfide crystallizes on every available surface — and the scaly-foot gastropod has evolved to incorporate that mineral rain into its own body.

No other animal does this. No other animal has learned to wear oxidation as a shell.

The scaly-foot snail does not grow its iron armor the way a turtle grows its carapace — from the inside out, by cellular instruction. Instead, it grows an organic scaffold and then *lets the environment deposit iron onto it*. The vent chemistry does the work. Time does the work. The animal simply persists at the boundary and allows transformation to occur.

This is OXIDIZE's core design philosophy: **the engine does not apply effects to a signal. It subjects a signal to time, and time does the rest.** The age accumulator ticks forward. The corrosion waveshaper intensifies. The erosion filter darkens. The entropy quantizer degrades resolution. The wobble increases. The dropout gates stutter. None of these are applied instantaneously — they are *accumulated*. The note ages, and the aging IS the synthesis.

### The Vent Field

Hydrothermal vents are the ocean's foundries. Superheated mineral-laden water erupts from the seafloor at 350°C, precipitating iron, copper, zinc, and sulfur compounds as it meets the 2°C ambient deep water. Black smoker chimneys grow centimeter by centimeter — mineral sediment accumulating over years into towers 60 meters tall. And then the vent dies. The chimney oxidizes. It corrodes. It collapses. A century of mineral accumulation returns to sediment on the abyssal plain.

OXIDIZE's Sediment FDN reverb is this process: the shared 4-delay-line FDN that accumulates across notes, with T60 ranging from 2 seconds to 300 seconds. Voices write their degraded signal into the sediment via three tap points — post-corrosion, post-entropy, post-dropout — and the sediment FDN holds the decay. Notes end, but their sediment remains. Like the mineral deposits around a vent field, the reverb tail carries the ghost-chemistry of every note that has passed through the engine.

### The Magnetic Shell

The iron sulfide in the scaly-foot snail's shell is magnetic. *Chrysomallon squamiferum* is the only known animal whose body responds to a magnet. In the deep ocean, surrounded by the geomagnetic field of the Earth's iron core, this animal's shell resonates with the planet's deepest signal.

OXIDIZE's coupling architecture resonates similarly. The `couplingAgeBoost` accelerates aging when another engine's signal feeds in — the external signal acts as a catalyst, speeding the oxidation. `couplingWobbleMod` destabilizes pitch stability. `couplingFilterMod` shifts the erosion floor. `couplingRingBuffer` applies the coupled engine's waveform as a ring modulation source. The engine is magnetically sensitive to its neighbors — not passively receiving their signal, but allowing their presence to accelerate its own transformation.

When OXIDIZE couples with another engine, the coupled signal doesn't blend. It *corrodes faster*. The external signal is the mineral-laden vent fluid. OXIDIZE is the shell that incorporates it.

### The Reverse Mode (Buchla's Insight)

In biology, oxidation is irreversible. Iron sulfide becomes iron oxide. The rust does not return to metal.

But OXIDIZE's reverse mode defies entropy. `reverseMode = true` starts the note at `preserveAmount` (maximum age) and decays toward pristine. The sound begins corroded, degraded, ancient — and as the note sustains, it clarifies. The rust flakes away. The iron returns to its unoxidized state. The signal becomes pristine.

This is the scaly-foot snail dreaming of the time before the vent field — before the iron deposited, before the sclerites mineralized, before the shell became magnetic. It is the note remembering what it was before time touched it.

The color sits at the intersection of the scaly-foot snail's iron-green sclerites and the mineral-stained water of the vent field. It reads as aged metal, deep water, chemical transformation — exactly what OXIDIZE sounds like.
