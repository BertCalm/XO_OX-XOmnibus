# Cookbook Production Spec

*Target: one medium-sized e-cookbook per Kitchen Collection quad, shipping with each quad release.*

## Scale

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Recipes per engine | 12 (appetizers, mains, sides, desserts) |
| Recipes per quad | 50 (12×4 engines + 2 coupling recipes) |
| Pages per cookbook | ~100 |
| Total across 6 quads | 300 recipes, ~600 pages |
| Current state | 4 per quad (24 total) — proof-of-concept validated |

## Cookbook Structure (per quad)

### Chapter 1: The Quad (8-10 pages)
- What these 4 engines are and how they relate
- The coupling mode explained through food
- How to read the recipes (the cooking↔synthesis parallel system)
- Quick reference: the 6 shared parameters and what they mean in this quad's cuisine

### Chapter 2-5: Engine Chapters (12 recipes each, ~20 pages each)
Each engine gets 12 recipes organized by course:
- **3 Starters/Appetizers** — quick, accessible, <30 min
- **4 Mains** — the core dishes, 30-90 min, full meals
- **2 Sides** — complementary dishes, pair with other engines' mains
- **2 Desserts/Drinks** — sweet or beverage, completes the meal
- **1 Signature Dish** — the engine's defining recipe, the one you'd cook to show someone what this engine sounds like

### Chapter 6: The Coupling Table (2 recipes)
Dishes that mirror specific coupling presets:
- Recipe A: two engines coupled cooperatively (the harmony dish)
- Recipe B: two engines in tension (the contrast dish)

### Chapter 7: Glossary & Index
- Full cooking↔synthesis translation table (from recipe-design-process.md)
- Index by ingredient, technique, cuisine, and engine
- "Go Deeper" section: recommended cookbooks, restaurants, music from each tradition

## Quality Gate (per recipe)

Every recipe must pass the 5 Commitments from `recipe-design-process.md`:
1. Tastes good (independent of any synth connection)
2. Accessible (under $30, standard kitchen, available ingredients)
3. Honors the culture (specific sourcing, no flattening)
4. Teaches something (food science + synthesis bridge)
5. Nourishes (real nutrition, not empty calories)

## Production Timeline

Each cookbook is produced during the quad's editorial window (2-3 weeks before release):

| Phase | Duration | Work |
|-------|----------|------|
| Week 1 | Research | Cultural deep-dive, source cookbook review, ingredient sourcing |
| Week 1-2 | Drafting | Write 50 recipes using Recipe Design Process pipeline |
| Week 2-3 | Review | Quill voice pass, cultural advisory check, accessibility audit |
| Week 3 | Layout | Format for PDF/EPUB, photography direction notes |
| Release day | Ship | Free PDF, Patreon EPUB, print-on-demand premium |

## Content Guidelines

### Recipe Naming
- Use original language name + English translation
- No puns or forced synth references in dish names
- The synthesis connection lives in the prose, not the title

### Photography Direction (for future)
- Warm, lived-in aesthetic (not sterile food photography)
- Show the cooking process, not just the finished plate
- Include the XOlokun UI in frame where natural (iPad on kitchen counter)
- Natural light preferred

### Cultural Advisory
- Name 3+ source cookbooks per regional cuisine (from recipe-design-process.md)
- Avoid the 5 appropriation traps
- Include "Go Deeper" recommendations per chapter
- Every recipe reviewed against its source tradition

## The Principle

*These are real cookbooks for real people. A producer who buys the Kitchen Collection gets instruments AND recipes. They cook dinner while their presets load. They learn about Senegalese cuisine while designing a clavinet patch. They understand fermentation because they made bone broth AND because they heard XOverworn reduce over a session. The food and the sound teach each other.*
