# XOmnibus Collections — Platform Vision

**Status:** Early vision
**Date:** March 2026
**Scope:** Meta-organization of all XOmnibus engines into themed Collections

---

## The Big Picture

XOmnibus is growing beyond its original engine roster. As new engines emerge — themed around cuisine, water, botany, and beyond — a higher-level organizational structure is needed. **Collections** are that structure.

A Collection is a themed family of engine quads that share:
- A **unifying metaphor** (culinary, celestial, scientific, etc.)
- A **community touchpoint** (cookbook, observatory log, research journal, etc.)
- A **Synesthesia Engine domain** (recipes, star charts, equations, etc.)
- A **visual identity** (color family, typography accent, UI treatment)
- **Internal coupling modes** unique to the collection's metaphor

Collections are NOT playlists or folders. They're **creative universes** — each one frames the act of sound design through a different lens, inviting a different kind of creative thinking.

---

## Collection Architecture

The original 26 engines are NOT one monolithic collection. They span creatures, oceans, materials, machines, forces, and aggression — each of those is its own creative universe. Forcing them into one "Founder's" bucket defeats the purpose of collections entirely.

Instead, the 26 are distributed across multiple collections based on their natural identity clusters. Some collections are purely original engines; others mix originals with new engines designed to fill out the collection's quads.

### Rules

1. **Every engine belongs to exactly one Collection.** No engine appears in multiple collections.
2. **Every Collection has at least 2 quads** (8 engines minimum). This ensures enough internal coupling variety to justify the collection's existence. Collections with fewer than 8 original engines can be filled out with new engines designed to complete the quads.
3. **Collections can cross-couple.** A Kitchen Essentials engine can couple with a Depths engine. Collections are organizational, not silos.
4. **Each Collection has a community touchpoint.** This is non-negotiable — every collection must have a real-world creative domain that connects to its presets.
5. **Original engines keep everything.** Parameter prefixes, accent colors, preset compatibility — nothing changes. Collection assignment is purely organizational.

## Collection Profiles

### Collection 1: The Depths (Aquatic / Creature)

**Engines:** 8 — OddfeliX, OddOscar, Ocelot, Owlfish, Oceanic, OceanDeep, Organon, OpenSky
**Metaphor:** The water column — from sunlit surface to abyssal trench. Creatures that swim through it, forces that shape it, skies that sit above it.
**Visual identity:** Deep blues, teals, bioluminescent accents. The aquatic mythology that started XOmnibus.
**Community touchpoint:** Marine journal — ocean science, underwater photography, creature illustrations, conservation stories → presets. "What does bioluminescence sound like?"
**Synesthesia domain:** Marine imagery, sonar data, ocean depth profiles, creature behaviors → presets.

| Quad | Engines | Identity |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Creatures** | OddfeliX (Neon Tetra Blue), OddOscar (Gill Pink), Ocelot (Tawny), Owlfish (Abyssal Gold) | Character instruments with animal souls. Each engine embodies its creature's movement, personality, habitat. |
| **Waters** | Oceanic (Phosphorescent Teal), OceanDeep (Trench Violet), Organon (Bioluminescent Cyan), OpenSky (Sunburst Orange) | Environment engines spanning the full water column — surface shimmer to crushing depth to the sky above. Organon bridges biology and environment (bioluminescence is both creature and place). |

*The Depths is the spiritual origin of XOmnibus. The aquatic mythology, the creature characters, the water column — this is where it all started. Making it its own collection honors that history while giving it room to grow. Future aquatic engines have a clear home.*

---

### Collection 2: The Atelier (Material / Craft)

**Engines:** 6 original + 2 future = 8 — Obsidian, Origami, Opal, Obscura, Oracle, Oblong + 2 TBD
**Metaphor:** The artist's workshop — raw materials being shaped, folded, cut, polished, photographed. Every engine is a substance or a tool.
**Visual identity:** Warm neutrals, material textures — stone grain, paper fiber, photographic silver. Tactile.
**Community touchpoint:** Maker journal — craftsmanship, material science, photography, sculpture, textile art → presets. "What does hand-thrown porcelain sound like?"
**Synesthesia domain:** Material properties (density, grain, transparency, flexibility), craft processes (folding, firing, developing, polishing) → presets.

| Quad | Engines | Identity |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Surfaces** | Obsidian (Crystal White), Origami (Vermillion Fold), Opal (Lavender), Obscura (Daguerreotype Silver) | Texture-forward engines. Each one IS a material — volcanic glass, folded paper, iridescent mineral, light-sensitive silver. The sound IS the substance. |
| **Tools** | Oracle (Prophecy Indigo), Oblong (Amber), + 2 future engines | Shaping instruments. Oracle's breakpoints are cuts and joints. Oblong's filters are lathes and planes. Two new engines would complete the toolkit — perhaps a forge/heat engine and a loom/weave engine. |

*Oracle and Oblong are the most architecturally unique original engines — breakpoint synthesis and filter sculpting. They don't fit in creature/ocean/force categories, but they're perfect as "tools" in a craftsperson's workshop. They SHAPE other sounds.*

### Collection 3: The Arcade (Machine / Process)

**Engines:** 5 original + 3 future = 8 — Overdub, Onset, Ostinato, Optic, Overworld + 3 TBD
**Metaphor:** The machine room — tape decks, drum machines, sequencers, oscilloscopes, game consoles. Every engine is a device with buttons, screens, and blinking lights.
**Visual identity:** Neon on dark, CRT phosphor glow, pixel art, hardware panel aesthetics.
**Community touchpoint:** Arcade log — retro computing, generative art, game design, hardware modding, creative coding → presets. "What does a Game Boy boot screen sound like?"
**Synesthesia domain:** Code snippets, pixel art, game mechanics, hardware schematics → presets. The AI interprets a BASIC program listing or a sprite sheet as sound parameters.

| Quad | Engines | Identity |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Machines** | Overdub (Olive), Onset (Electric Blue), Ostinato (Firelight Orange), Optic (Phosphor Green) | Functional engines — each one DOES something specific. Overdub layers. Onset attacks. Ostinato repeats. Optic visualizes. These are tools masquerading as instruments. |
| **Worlds** | Overworld (Neon Green), + 3 future engines | Environment engines — Overworld's retro gaming DNA. Three new engines could fill this out: a chiptune engine, a glitch engine, a circuit-bent engine. The Arcade needs more cabinets. |

*The Arcade is the most "functional" collection — these engines are about PROCESS more than timbre. They're the workshop tools that happen to make interesting sounds. Overworld's gaming identity seeds the collection's retro-tech aesthetic.*

**Engines:** 8 — Odyssey, Orbital, Ouroboros, Obese, Overbite, Oblique, Ouïe + 1 future engine

**Community touchpoint:** Field journal — experimental art, extreme music, noise culture, physics visualizations, boundary-pushing performance → presets. "What does a controlled demolition sound like?"
**Synesthesia domain:** Physics simulations, extreme sports footage, abstract expressionist paintings, mathematical chaos → presets.

| Quad | Engines | Identity |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Forces** | Odyssey (Violet), Orbital (Warm Red), Ouroboros (Strange Attractor Red) + 1 future engine | Dynamic system engines — voyage/drift, celestial mechanics, self-eating feedback. A fourth engine (gravity? magnetism? entropy?) completes the physics quartet. |
| **Edges** | Obese (Hot Pink), Overbite (Fang White), Oblique (Prism Violet), Ouïe (Hammerhead Steel) | The aggressive quad. Saturation, bite, prismatic refraction, hammer percussion. These engines push past polite. They break things on purpose. |

*The Frontier is for users who want to go further than "nice sounds." Forces provides the intellectual edge (chaos theory, orbital mechanics). Edges provides the sonic edge (distortion, aggression). Together they define the boundary of what XOmnibus can do.*

## The Community Model

Each Collection's community touchpoint isn't marketing — it's a **creative feedback loop** where real-world creative inputs and sound design inspire each other bidirectionally.

### Per-Collection Interaction Points

```
COLLECTION          COMMUNITY INPUT           COMMUNITY OUTPUT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The Depths          Ocean photography          Marine journal entries
                    Marine science, sonar      Creature + environment presets
                    "What does the abyss       Depth-based preset series
                     sound like?"

The Atelier         Craft photos, materials    Maker journal entries
                    "What does marble          Material + technique presets
                     sound like?"
                    Texture samples

The Arcade          Pixel art, code snippets   Arcade log entries
                    Game mechanics, hardware    Retro + generative presets
                    "What does Pong sound like?"

The Frontier        Physics sims, chaos viz    Field journal entries
                    Extreme art, noise culture Experimental presets
                    "What does a black hole    Boundary-pushing patches
                     sound like?"

Kitchen Essentials  Real recipes               Cookbook (ebook, web, print?)
                    Food photos                Preset + recipe pairings
                    "This dish sounds like..."  Seasonal recipe/preset drops

Constellation       Star charts                Observatory log entries
                    "Tonight's sky"            Event-based preset drops
                    Astrophotography           Photo + preset pairings

Laboratory          Equations, papers          Research journal entries
                    "What does ___ sound like?" Theory + preset pairings
                    Data sets                  Data sonification presets
```

### The Synesthesia Engine Unifies Everything

The Synesthesia Engine (documented in `culinary_collection_overview.md`) is the technical bridge:

```
ANY CREATIVE INPUT ──→ SYNESTHESIA ENGINE ──→ XOMNIBUS PRESET
     (recipe)              (AI model)           (.xometa)
     (star chart)          maps input to
     (equation)            6D Sonic DNA
     (poem)                selects engines
     (color palette)       sets parameters
     (photograph)
```

The engine learns from community ratings — as users vote on AI-generated presets, the mapping model improves. Over time, the community collectively trains a synesthesia model that reflects shared human intuitions about cross-sensory mapping.

### Community Growth Flywheel

```
New Collection launches
        │
        ▼
Community touchpoint activates (cookbook, observatory, journal)
        │
        ▼
Users submit creative inputs (recipes, star charts, equations)
        │
        ▼
Synesthesia Engine generates presets from inputs
        │
        ▼
Users rate, tweak, and share AI presets
        │
        ▼
Best community presets become official "Community Picks"
        │
        ▼
Community Picks inspire new real-world inputs ("that preset made me cook THIS")
        │
        ▼
Cycle deepens — each Collection's library grows from both directions
```

## Open Questions

1. **Organon placement** — Currently in The Depths (Waters quad). Bioluminescence bridges creature biology and ocean environment. But Organon's "entropy" parameter and biological systems focus could also argue for The Frontier (Forces). Where does it feel most at home?
2. **Ocelot in The Depths?** — Ocelot is a jungle cat, not aquatic. It's grouped with Creatures because all animal-identity engines are there. But should Creatures be renamed to something less aquatic-specific? "Bestiary" perhaps?
3. **Collection visual identity** — How different should collections look? Subtle (same Gallery Model, different accent palette) or dramatic (different UI shell per collection)?
4. **Cross-collection coupling** — Do coupling modes change when engines from different collections interact? Or is coupling universal?
5. **Collection pricing** — Are the four original-engine collections (Depths, Atelier, Arcade, Frontier) the free base, with Culinary and future collections as expansions? Or does everything stay free?
6. **Growth slot engines** — The 6 slots (2 Atelier Tools, 3 Arcade Worlds, 1 Frontier Forces) should be designed TO their collection's metaphor, not just slotted in. What craft tools, retro devices, and physical forces are missing?
7. **Synesthesia Engine MVP** — What's the simplest version that proves the concept? Probably: recipe text → 6D Sonic DNA → engine/preset suggestion. No parameter-level AI at first.
8. **Constellation and Laboratory timing** — These are far future. Should they even be documented now, or does that distract from shipping Kitchen Essentials?
9. **Collection-specific presets vs cross-collection presets** — Do presets stay within their collection, or can a preset load engines from multiple collections? If cross-collection, the browser UX needs to handle that.
