# XOmnibus Collections — Platform Vision

**Status:** Early vision
**Date:** March 2026
**Scope:** Meta-organization of all XOmnibus engines into themed Collections

---

## The Big Picture

XOmnibus is growing beyond its original engine roster. As new engines emerge — themed around cuisine, water, botany, and beyond — a higher-level organizational structure is needed. **Collections** are that structure.

A Collection is a themed family of engine quads that share:
- A **unifying metaphor** (culinary, celestial, scientific, etc.)
- A **community touchpoint** (cookbook, observatory log, research journal, etc.)
- A **Synesthesia Engine domain** (recipes, star charts, equations, etc.)
- A **visual identity** (color family, typography accent, UI treatment)
- **Internal coupling modes** unique to the collection's metaphor

Collections are NOT playlists or folders. They're **creative universes** — each one frames the act of sound design through a different lens, inviting a different kind of creative thinking.

### Rules

1. **Every engine belongs to exactly one Collection.** No engine appears in multiple collections.
2. **Every Collection has at least 2 quads** (8 engines minimum). This ensures enough internal coupling variety to justify the collection's existence.
3. **Collections can cross-couple.** A Kitchen Essentials engine can couple with a Founder's Original engine. Collections are organizational, not silos.
4. **Each Collection has a community touchpoint.** This is non-negotiable — every collection must have a real-world creative domain that connects to its presets.
5. **The Founder's Originals are retroactively organized into quads** from the existing 26-engine roster. This gives the original engines the same structural clarity as newer collections.

## The Community Model

Each Collection's community touchpoint isn't marketing — it's a **creative feedback loop** where real-world creative inputs and sound design inspire each other bidirectionally.

### Per-Collection Interaction Points

```
COLLECTION          COMMUNITY INPUT           COMMUNITY OUTPUT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Founder's Originals Reference tracks          Patches, sound design tutorials
                    "Make it sound like..."   Technique videos

Kitchen Essentials  Real recipes              Cookbook (ebook, web, print?)
                    Food photos               Preset + recipe pairings
                    "This dish sounds like..." Seasonal recipe/preset drops

Constellation       Star charts               Observatory log entries
                    "Tonight's sky"           Event-based preset drops
                    Astrophotography          Photo + preset pairings

Laboratory          Equations, papers         Research journal entries
                    "What does ___ sound like?" Theory + preset pairings
                    Data sets                 Data sonification presets
```

### The Synesthesia Engine Unifies Everything

The Synesthesia Engine (documented in `culinary_collection_overview.md`) is the technical bridge:

```
ANY CREATIVE INPUT ──→ SYNESTHESIA ENGINE ──→ XOMNIBUS PRESET
     (recipe)              (AI model)           (.xometa)
     (star chart)          maps input to
     (equation)            6D Sonic DNA
     (poem)                selects engines
     (color palette)       sets parameters
     (photograph)
```

The engine learns from community ratings — as users vote on AI-generated presets, the mapping model improves. Over time, the community collectively trains a synesthesia model that reflects shared human intuitions about cross-sensory mapping.

### Community Growth Flywheel

```
New Collection launches
        │
        ▼
Community touchpoint activates (cookbook, observatory, journal)
        │
        ▼
Users submit creative inputs (recipes, star charts, equations)
        │
        ▼
Synesthesia Engine generates presets from inputs
        │
        ▼
Users rate, tweak, and share AI presets
        │
        ▼

        │
        ▼
Community Picks inspire new real-world inputs ("that preset made me cook THIS")
        │
        ▼
Cycle deepens — each Collection's library grows from both directions
```

## Founder's Originals — Quad Retrofit

The original 26 engines were designed as individuals. Grouping them into quads retroactively serves several purposes:

1. **Navigational consistency** — users learn the quad structure from Kitchen Essentials, then find the same structure everywhere
2. **Coupling discovery** — quad groupings surface natural coupling partnerships
3. **Preset organization** — presets can be tagged by quad, not just by engine
4. **Visual coherence** — quad-level color families help users parse the engine browser

### Important: Retroactive Grouping is Gentle

The original engines keep their individual identities, accent colors, and parameter prefixes. Quad grouping adds a layer of organization WITHOUT changing anything about the engines themselves. It's a lens, not a refactor.

A possible approach:

**Organize by synthesis affinity** (engines that share DSP approaches or timbral families):

| Quad Name | Engines | Shared DNA |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Creatures** | OddfeliX, OddOscar, Ocelot, Owlfish | Character-driven, animal-identity, personality-forward |
| **Forces** | Odyssey, Ouroboros, Orbital, Organon | Dynamic systems — chaos, gravity, entropy, biology |
| **Surfaces** | Obsidian, Origami, Opal, Obscura | Material-forward — crystal, paper, grain, daguerreotype |
| **Machines** | Overdub, Onset, Ostinato, Optic | Functional — delay, drums, sequencer, visualizer |
| **Spaces** | Overworld, Oceanic, OceanDeep, OpenSky | Environment — retro, deep sea, abyss, atmosphere |
| **Edges** | Obese, Overbite, Oblique, Ouïe | Aggressive — saturation, bite, prism, hammer |
| **Waypoints** | Oracle, Oblong | Unique engines that anchor routing — breakpoint and filter |

*7 provisional quads. Some only have 2 engines — these could absorb future engines or merge. Oracle and Oblong might pair with 2 future engines to form a complete quad.*

## Open Questions

1. **Founder's Original quads** — The retroactive grouping needs community input. Do users already think of certain engines as belonging together? Survey the existing user base.
2. **Collection visual identity** — How different should collections look? Subtle (same Gallery Model, different accent palette) or dramatic (different UI shell per collection)?
3. **Cross-collection coupling** — Do coupling modes change when engines from different collections interact? Or is coupling universal?
4. **Collection pricing** — Founder's Originals are the free base. Are new collections expansions? Or does everything stay free?
5. **Synesthesia Engine MVP** — What's the simplest version that proves the concept? Probably: recipe text → 6D Sonic DNA → engine/preset suggestion. No parameter-level AI at first.
6. **Constellation and Laboratory timing** — These are far future. Should they even be documented now, or does that distract from shipping Kitchen Essentials?
