## Inspirations & Sonic DNA

### Pioneer Synth Concept: Subharmonic Synthesis (Mixtur-Trautonium)

The core innovation. Friedrich Trautwein invented the Trautonium in 1929. Oskar Sala perfected it over 50+ years into the Mixtur-Trautonium (1948), developing **subharmonic synthesis** — generating frequencies BELOW the fundamental at fractional divisions (1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5 of the played frequency). The "Mixtur" technique combines 4 subharmonics into rich, otherworldly timbres.

Key facts:
- Sala created the bird attack sounds for Hitchcock's "The Birds" (1963) using subharmonic combinations
- Monophonic with extreme expression via pressure-sensitive wire interface
- Almost no modern synthesizer implements true subharmonic generation
- The Trautonium goes DOWN where every other synth goes UP

For XOwlfish, subharmonic synthesis means the engine generates frequencies that *dive below* the played note — into the bathypelagic zone of the frequency spectrum. The Mixtur technique becomes the creature's voice: combining subharmonic divisions into timbres impossible through conventional harmonic synthesis.

## Core Synthesis Architecture

### The Organism Model

XOwlfish is NOT a layered engine (like OCELOT) or a serial effects chain (like OCEANIC). It's an **organism** — five interdependent organs that function as one body. Each organ influences the others:

- Stress (velocity) triggers Armor, which changes what Diet receives
- Depth (subharmonic mix) changes what Optics can compress
- Feeding (grain activity) modulates the Abyss reverb character
- Portamento (Solitary Genus) morphs ALL organs during glide, not just pitch

### Organ Detail

#### SOLITARY GENUS — Monophonic Nervous System

The voice management layer. Deliberately monophonic — the defining architectural choice.

- **Mono allocation:** Only one note sounds at a time. New notes steal immediately.
- **Legato mode:** When playing connected notes, the envelope doesn't retrigger — only pitch changes. The creature glides rather than re-attacking.
- **Portamento:** Heavy glide time (0–2s). But this isn't simple pitch interpolation. During portamento, ALL organs morph:
  - Filter cutoff sweeps (Optics adjusts to new depth)
  - Grain density shifts (Diet changes feeding behavior)
  - Sub-bass body frequency tracks (habitat pressure changes)
  - Compression threshold adjusts
- **Morph amount** (`owl_morphGlide`): Controls how much non-pitch parameters morph during glide. At 0, only pitch glides. At 1, the whole organism moves.

#### ABYSS HABITAT — Subharmonic Oscillator

The sound source. Inspired by Oskar Sala's Mixtur-Trautonium.

- **Fundamental oscillator:** Standard pitched oscillator at MIDI frequency (sine/triangle blend for purity)
- **Subharmonic generator:** Produces up to 4 simultaneous subharmonic divisions of the fundamental:
  - Division ratios selectable: 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5, 1/6, 1/7, 1/8
  - Each with independent level control
  - Implementation: frequency division via phase accumulator stepping at 1/N rate
- **Mixtur blend** (`owl_mixtur`): Controls how the 4 subharmonics combine — Sala's technique:
  - Low values = subharmonics layered cleanly (organ-like)
  - High values = subharmonics interact through waveshaping (growling, alive)
- **Sub-bass body** (`owl_bodyFreq`, `owl_bodyLevel`): A separate low-frequency oscillator providing constant habitat pressure — the ambient 300-atmosphere weight of the bathypelagic zone. Not pitched to MIDI — it's the environment, not the creature's voice.
- **Waveform:** Fundamental is sine/triangle. Subharmonics use triangle/saw blend for harmonic content that the Optics stage can then compress and filter.

#### OWL OPTICS — Extreme Compressor + Resonant Filter

The perceptual system. The owl's eyes don't create light — they amplify the faintest photons into visible signal. The compressor does the same: pulls quiet harmonics up into audibility.

- **Compressor:**
  - Ratio: 1:1 to infinity:1 (full limiter mode)
  - Threshold: -60dB to 0dB (can compress extremely quiet signals)
  - Attack: 0.1ms to 100ms
  - Release: 10ms to 1000ms
  - Makeup gain: automatic (computed from ratio + threshold — no user parameter needed)
  - At extreme settings, this reveals micro-details in the subharmonic output that would normally be inaudible — the owlfish seeing individual photons
- **Resonant lowpass filter:**
  - CytomicSVF-based (consistent with XOceanus DSP library)
  - Cutoff: 20 Hz to 20 kHz (can be tuned to subharmonic frequencies)
  - Resonance: 0 to 1 (self-oscillation at max — the filter sings)
  - Key tracking: 0 to 100%
  - The filter can focus on specific subharmonic divisions — "looking" at particular frequency bands

#### DIET — Micro-Granular Processor

The metabolic system. The owlfish feeds on copepods — organisms measured in millimeters. The granular processor "feeds" on the audio signal with ultra-short grains.

- **Grain size:** 2–10ms (deliberately much shorter than OPAL's 20–500ms range)
- **Grain density:** 10–200 grains/second
- **Pitch scatter:** 0–12 semitones (random pitch offset per grain)
- **Feed rate** (`owl_feedRate`): How fast the grain reader advances through the buffer — the creature's appetite. Low = slow, contemplative feeding. High = voracious consumption.
- **Grain window:** Hann window for smooth edges (no clicks from tiny grains)
- **Mix:** 0 = bypass (no feeding). 1 = full granular output.
- **Distinction from OPAL:** OPAL creates ambient grain clouds from longer windows. OWLFISH's Diet is predatory — ultra-short grains that consume and re-synthesize the signal as crawling, alive texture. Different purpose, different scale.

#### SACRIFICIAL ARMOR — Velocity-Triggered Stress Response

The defense mechanism. When the owlfish is attacked, it sheds bony scales as a distraction. In synthesis: when you play hard, the current audio is captured, fragmented, and scattered — while the main signal ducks.

- **Trigger:** MIDI velocity exceeds `owl_armorThreshold` (0–127)
- **On trigger:**
  1. Current audio buffer is captured (snapshot of the last ~50ms)
  2. Buffer is fragmented into randomized grains with pitch scatter
  3. Grains are played back through a delay line with feedback
  4. Main signal ducks by `owl_armorDuck` amount (0–100%)
  5. Armor grain cloud decays over `owl_armorDecay` time (50ms–2s)
- **Musical effect:** Hard strikes cause the sound to momentarily shatter into a cloud of echoing fragments while the sustained tone dips — then the creature re-emerges. Soft playing = smooth, uninterrupted voice. The dynamic range of expression is enormous.
- **Armor parameters:**
  - `owl_armorThreshold` — velocity sensitivity (64 = half-hard triggers, 127 = only hardest)
  - `owl_armorDecay` — fragment cloud persistence
  - `owl_armorScatter` — pitch randomization of fragments (0–24 semitones)
  - `owl_armorDuck` — main signal attenuation during shedding
  - `owl_armorDelay` — delay time for fragment echoes (50ms–500ms)

#### ABYSS REVERB — The Habitat Space

The environment. Not a generic reverb — designed specifically for the bathypelagic zone.

- **Character:** Dark, massive, slow. Heavy LP damping on the tail (no high frequencies survive at depth). Long pre-delay (sound travels slowly through dense water).
- **Size:** Scaled from "underwater cave" to "open ocean trench"
- **Damping:** Heavy by default — the abyss absorbs everything above the low-mids
- **Pre-delay:** 20ms–200ms (simulating sound propagation through dense water)
- **Modulation:** Subtle pitch modulation on reverb tail (water currents shifting the space)
- **Diet feedback:** Reverb size subtly responds to Diet grain density — more feeding activity = larger perceived space (the creature's metabolic energy fills the habitat)

## Coupling Interface Design

### OWLFISH as Target (receiving from other engines)

| Coupling Type | What XOwlfish Does | Musical Effect |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| `AudioToWavetable` | External audio enters the Diet grain buffer as prey | Any engine's sound consumed and re-synthesized as micro-granular texture. **THE killer coupling** — the owlfish EATS other engines. |
| `AudioToFM` | External audio frequency-modulates the subharmonic oscillator | Deep, growling, unpredictable sub-bass modulation. Organic FM below the fundamental. |
| `AmpToFilter` | Source amplitude drives Owl Optics filter cutoff | Drum hits open the creature's eyes — rhythmic brightness from darkness. |
| `RhythmToBlend` | Source rhythm pattern drives sub/fundamental mix | External rhythm shifts between surface and deep — the creature dives and surfaces with the beat. |
| `EnvToDecay` | Source envelope controls armor decay time | External dynamics control how long shed armor persists — cross-engine stress response. |
| `EnvToMorph` | Source envelope drives Mixtur blend (`owl_mixtur`) | External dynamics control subharmonic interaction — crescendos in other engines make the creature's voice growl and thicken. |
| `LFOToPitch` | Source LFO modulates fundamental pitch (subharmonics follow) | Cross-engine vibrato. Subharmonic ratios are preserved because they track the fundamental — the whole creature wobbles together. |

**Primary coupling:** `AudioToWavetable` — feeding other engines' audio into the micro-granular Diet stage. ORBITAL's bright additive partials consumed as prey and re-synthesized as deep, granular texture. OCELOT's ecosystem output eaten and digested. ODDFELIX's FM percussion ground into copepod-sized grains. The owlfish is the apex predator of the coupling matrix.

### OWLFISH as Source (sending to other engines)

`getSampleForCoupling()` returns: post-reverb mono output (the full creature voice including habitat reverb).

**Good receiving engines:**
- **ORBITAL** — Owlfish subharmonics + Orbital additive partials = full harmonic + subharmonic spectrum. The definitive pairing.
- **OCELOT** — Owlfish as the sub-bass foundation under the ecosystem. The floor below the Floor.
- **OVERBITE** — Two bass engines coupled: Overbite's belly/bite duality + Owlfish's abyssal subharmonics. Bass apocalypse.
- **OCEANIC** — Owlfish's creature voice through the chromatophore pedalboard — deep-sea sound revealed in new spectral colors.
- **OVERDUB** — Subharmonic bass through dub delay. Pressure dub.

### Coupling types OWLFISH should NOT receive

| Type | Why |
|------|-----|
| `AmpToChoke` | Choking a monophonic creature kills the only voice — no musical recovery possible |
| `PitchToPitch` | Conflicts with subharmonic generation — external pitch tracking would fight the subharmonic ratios |
