## Top 5 Most Urgent Gaps

Ranked by severity (pole depletion below threshold, or identity tension):

### 1. Aether / space XLOW — 4.6% ⚠️ CRITICAL
Only ~34 Aether presets have space ≤ 0.15. Aether is almost entirely open, expansive, reverberant. A claustrophobic or close-contact Aether preset is nearly nonexistent. Recommended fill: 15–20 Aether presets with space = 0.05–0.12, high aggression XLOW (consistent with mood), movement low, density moderate-to-high. Think: intimate ritual spaces, close-mic acoustic transcendence, whispering-in-your-ear textures.

### 2. Entangled / movement XLOW — 6.8% (thinning)
With 3,700 presets, Entangled's 6.8% movement XLOW = ~252 presets — thin for the fleet's largest mood. Completely static, frozen, motionless Entangled textures are underrepresented. Recommended fill: 30–50 presets with movement = 0.00–0.12. Coupling presets work well here (two engines locking into a static tableau).

### 3. Entangled overall cosine diversity — 0.1536 (lowest in fleet)
No single pole is critically gapped, but the entire Entangled mood center-hugs. All six dimensions show compressed distribution. Recommended fill: a targeted extreme-zone injection of 50–80 presets spread across all 6 dimensions at both poles — prioritizing combinations not already represented (e.g., low-brightness + low-warmth + high-aggression, or high-movement + high-density + low-space).

### 4. Aether / warmth XHIGH — 8.2% (approaching threshold)
Aether skews cold and dark. Only 8.2% of Aether presets are warm-high. Warm transcendent textures (sun-drenched pads, glowing drones) are underrepresented. Recommended fill: 10–15 Aether presets with warmth ≥ 0.88, space still high (Aether identity), aggression low.

### 5. Aether / movement XHIGH — 8.2% (approaching threshold)
Aether is mostly still. Only 8.2% reach high movement. Kinetic-transcendence Aether presets (spiraling ascension, restless ether, turbulent void) are scarce. Recommended fill: 10–15 Aether presets with movement ≥ 0.88, space high, aggression low. These sit at the Aether↔Flux border and would be musically interesting edge cases.
