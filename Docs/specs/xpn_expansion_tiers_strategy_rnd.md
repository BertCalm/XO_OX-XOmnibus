# XPN Expansion Pack Tier Strategy

**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Author**: XO_OX R&D Session
**Status**: R&D spec — not yet ratified

---

## Overview

XO_OX expansion packs ship as `.xpn` bundles for the Akai MPC ecosystem. This document defines the three commercial tiers — SIGNAL, FORM, and DOCTRINE — covering content requirements, DNA coverage standards, pricing rationale, and release strategy. The tier system creates a coherent product ladder: free discovery at one end, premium multi-engine collections at the other, with a healthy commercial middle.

---

## 1. The Three Tiers

### SIGNAL — Free / $9–12
The gateway product. SIGNAL packs are designed for first contact: a producer encounters XO_OX for the first time and needs a low-friction reason to trust the sound design philosophy. A single engine, curated to its most essential and immediately usable programs.

- **Engine scope**: Single engine only
- **Program count**: 8–16 programs
- **Bonus content**: None — no README, no MPCE_SETUP.md, no field guide
- **Purpose**: Discovery and trust-building. These packs must stand alone as a satisfying micro-experience, not a teaser that withholds the good material.
- **Pricing logic**: Free tier (when used as a promotional gateway) or $9–12 when sold standalone. Below the impulse-buy threshold for working producers.

### FORM — $15–18
The core commercial tier. FORM packs represent the primary revenue vehicle — fully realized, professionally presented single-engine (or engine-pair) releases with complete documentation and cover art.

- **Engine scope**: Single engine, or a two-engine pairing with deliberate coupling rationale
- **Program count**: 24–40 programs
- **Bonus content**: Cover art (required), README.md (required), MPCE_SETUP.md where the engine benefits from macro explanation
- **DNA coverage**: Full (see Section 3)
- **Purpose**: The product a returning customer buys after a positive SIGNAL experience. Must feel complete — not a sampler.

### DOCTRINE — $25–35
The premium tier. DOCTRINE packs are multi-engine, coupling-optimized releases that demonstrate the full XO_OX philosophy. These are long-form products for committed users and collectors.

- **Engine scope**: 2–4 engines, with coupling presets that only make sense across the bundle
- **Program count**: 40–60+ programs
- **Bonus content**: Cover art (required), extended README with production notes (required), field guide article (required), bonus sample stems where applicable, Seance PDF optional
- **DNA coverage**: Full spectrum with enforced range minimums (see Section 3)
- **Purpose**: The definitive release for a collection arc or a major engine pairing. Comparable in value proposition to boutique sample label maxi-packs.

## 2. Tier Decision Criteria

Assign a pack to a tier by evaluating four factors:

1. **Engine depth** — How much expressive range does the engine have? Deep engines (OPAL, OVERLAP, OUTWIT) warrant FORM or DOCTRINE. Percussive or utility engines may be appropriate at SIGNAL.
2. **Sound design effort** — Program count and DNA breadth determine the floor. A 12-program pack with shallow coverage cannot be FORM regardless of engine depth.
3. **Bonus content readiness** — DOCTRINE requires a field guide article and extended notes. If that content doesn't exist or isn't being written, the pack cannot be DOCTRINE.
4. **Price sensitivity** — Collection packs targeting new customers should lead with SIGNAL. DOCTRINE requires an established audience who already trusts the brand.

---

## 3. DNA Coverage Requirements Per Tier

All programs carry Sonic DNA metadata across six dimensions: feliX/Oscar polarity, bright/warm tonal axis, density, movement, aggression, and space. Coverage requirements per tier:

- **SIGNAL**: Minimum 3 of the 4 feliX/Oscar × bright/warm quadrants represented. DNA metadata required but range enforcement is relaxed.
- **FORM**: All 4 quadrants present. All 6 DNA dimensions must appear across the program set (no dimension may be absent). Recommended minimum spread of 0.3 per dimension.
- **DOCTRINE**: Full spectrum coverage. Minimum range of 0.5 per dimension across the full program set. Coupling presets must demonstrate cross-engine DNA interaction (e.g., an OPAL pad layered against an OVERLAP texture with opposing feliX/Oscar values).

## 4. Program Count Guidelines

| Tier     | Minimum | Recommended | Maximum |
|----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| SIGNAL   | 8       | 12          | 16      |
| FORM     | 24      | 32          | 40      |
| DOCTRINE | 40      | 50          | 64      |

Programs above the maximum should be split into a companion pack rather than bloating a single release. Larger isn't better — curation density is the quality signal.

## 6. Pricing Rationale

The tier price points are calibrated against the broader boutique sample market:

- **Drum Broker**: $15–25 per pack — mid-tier competition, primarily drum kits, less synthesis depth
- **MSXII**: $25–40 — closest philosophical peer, high-quality synthesis packs with strong DNA curation
- **Output**: $49+ — subscription or premium bundles, DAW-integrated, different market

XO_OX FORM at $15–18 sits slightly below MSXII, appropriate while building catalog depth. DOCTRINE at $25–35 enters MSXII territory and must deliver comparable perceived value — hence the field guide article and production notes requirements. These aren't padding; they're the justification for the price point.

SIGNAL as a free or sub-$12 product has no direct competition concern. Its purpose is funnel entry, not margin.

## 7. Collection Strategy

A standard collection arc ships four packs across the three tiers:

**One SIGNAL + Two FORM + One DOCTRINE**

The SIGNAL leads — free or promotional, tied to the most immediately accessible engine in the collection. The two FORM packs cover the collection's primary engine identities. The DOCTRINE closes the arc with a multi-engine synthesis that rewards customers who bought into the full collection.

Example structure for a hypothetical Water Collection arc:
- SIGNAL: OCEANDEEP (8 programs, bass-forward, free)
- FORM: OPENSKY (32 programs, shimmer and lift)
- FORM: OVERLAP (32 programs, FDN texture)
- DOCTRINE: DEPTHS BUNDLE — OCEANDEEP + OVERLAP coupled (50 programs, field guide, production notes)

Collections should be conceived as arcs from the start. Retrofitting DOCTRINE coupling logic onto packs designed independently produces weaker results.

---

## 8. Release Cadence Recommendations

| Tier     | Target per Quarter |
|----------|--------------------|
| SIGNAL   | 2–3               |
| FORM     | 3–4               |
| DOCTRINE | 1                 |

DOCTRINE releases are events, not routine drops. One per quarter maintains scarcity and gives production time for the required field guide content. FORM packs sustain revenue between DOCTRINE releases. SIGNAL packs can ship opportunistically — tied to engine launches, seasonal promotions, or community milestones.

Total quarterly output target: **6–8 packs across all tiers**, with the DOCTRINE release as the anchor.
