## 2. OCTOPUS Sonic Character

OCTOPUS presets do not sound broken. They sound decided — just not by a human.

The 8 semi-autonomous arms each carry their own pitch offset, envelope curve, and modulation phase. A soft velocity hit might wake 2 arms. A hard hit wakes all 8. The result is not a chord — it is a deployment. Arms converge on a tonal target from different arrival angles, creating that signature OCTOPUS smear-then-focus.

Chromatophore flashes are color modulation: fast pigment cells that open and close over the sound. In patch terms this means timbral brightness surges that are not random — they follow the arm activity. Busy arms = more flash. Resting arms = skin goes dark and still.

Ink cloud is the disruptor. It is not reverb. It is a textural signal bloat — the harmonic equivalent of total camouflage. Used sparingly it adds drama. Used fully it swallows the patch and leaves only pressure.

The guiding principle: **inhuman but purposeful**. Every strange thing the OCTOPUS does has a logic. It just is not your logic.

## 3. Pack Structure — 16 Pads

**Row A — Arms (Pads 1–4)**
One arm configuration per pad, ascending deployment.
- Pad 1: Single arm. Narrow, focused, almost melodic. The OCTOPUS thinking.
- Pad 2: Dual arm. Slight phase divergence, first signs of independent motion.
- Pad 3: Trio arm. Tonal smear begins. Envelope arrivals disagree.
- Pad 4: All 8 arms. Full deployment. Dense, kinetic, entirely alien.

**Row B — Chromatophores (Pads 5–8)**
Flash pattern is the variable; arm configuration held at 4-arm mid.
- Pad 5: Slow ripple — long chromatophore cycle, tidal brightness shift.
- Pad 6: Fast strobe — rapid pigment pulse, rhythmically aggressive.
- Pad 7: Wave — brightness travels arm-to-arm, left to right.
- Pad 8: Chaos burst — unpredictable flash, maximum alien affect.

**Row C — Ink Cloud (Pads 9–12)**
Textural disruption arc from clean to total occlusion.
- Pad 9: Trace ink — faint harmonic haze, signal still readable.
- Pad 10: Rising cloud — density climbing, edges starting to blur.
- Pad 11: Full release — signal bloat, harmonic identity softening.
- Pad 12: Blackout — complete ink saturation, pure pressure remains.

**Row D — Full Intelligence (Pads 13–16)**
All systems engaged. These are showcase presets.
- Pad 13: Hunting — directional movement, arms in coordinated sweep.
- Pad 14: Display — chromatophore performance at peak, visual/sonic spectacle.
- Pad 15: Escape — ink cloud + arm retraction, velocity-reactive chaos.
- Pad 16: Deep intelligence — slow, vast, 8-arm harmonic architecture with long chromatophore cycle. The OCTOPUS at rest but aware.

## 4. Preset Design Philosophy

Velocity = arm count deployment. Low velocity wakes 1–2 arms. High velocity activates the full 8. This means every pad in the pack is dynamically a different instrument. A soft hit on Pad 16 sounds like a thought. A hard hit sounds like a decision.

Presets should resist resolution. They should move toward a tonal target but never quite arrive before the next hit. The OCTOPUS is always mid-gesture.

Do not humanize the modulation. Resist LFO shapes that feel like breath or heartbeat. Arm phase offsets should be prime-number ratios. Chromatophore timing should feel biological, not musical.
