# XPN Affiliate & Partnership R&D
**XO_OX Pack Distribution — Year 1 Strategy**

---

## 1. Gumroad Affiliate Program

Gumroad has built-in affiliate support. Any creator can generate a unique affiliate link for a product; Gumroad handles tracking and splits the payout automatically at checkout. Setup takes about 10 minutes per product: go to the product dashboard, enable affiliates, set the commission rate, then share the affiliate invite link.

**Commission rate**: 20% is the right starting point. Below 15% and no one bothers. Above 25% and margins shrink fast on a $15-20 pack. 20% is credible and common in the sample pack space.

**Who to recruit**:
- MPC tutorial YouTubers (channels with 5K–50K subscribers are the sweet spot — big enough to have reach, small enough that a free pack + 20% commission is genuinely interesting to them)
- MPC-Forums power users who post gear reviews and workflow threads
- Music production bloggers who already link out to packs in their posts

Outreach: send a free copy of the pack, explain the affiliate link takes 2 minutes to set up, mention the commission. Keep the email short. Follow up once after 2 weeks if no response.

---

## 2. Cross-Studio Collaboration

A co-branded pack means another studio contributes raw sounds; XO_OX handles processing, engine integration, and XOceanus export. The result is something neither studio could make alone.

**Revenue split**: Start with 50/50. Adjust if one side is doing dramatically more work.

**Finding the right partner**: Look for complementary skills (they record live instruments or have a unique recording setup; XO_OX brings synthesis and engine depth), a shared audience (they make MPC content), and no direct overlap (they don't already sell XPN packs). MPC-Forums, Looperman, and Beat Academy communities are good starting points. A small studio with one strong niche pack and an active following is more useful than a larger studio with competing products.

---

## 3. Hardware Bundle Partnerships (Akai)

Bundling a free pack with new MPC hardware is the highest-visibility distribution channel but the hardest to access. Akai requires a formal business relationship, quality review, legal agreement, and likely a volume commitment (thousands of units). There is no cold-email path to this.

Realistic timeline: Year 2 at the earliest, after the catalog has 5+ packs, documented download numbers, and some press coverage to point to. The path runs through their content partnerships or distributor relations team, not general support. Put this on the radar but do not invest time in it during Year 1.

---

## 4. Music Production Blog Placements

"Best MPC Expansion Packs 2026" roundup posts rank well and convert. Getting included requires: a free review copy sent with no strings attached, a short pitch (2–3 sentences on what makes the pack distinct), and a press kit.

**What makes a press kit useful**: 3–4 high-resolution screenshots (MPC display + program browser), a 60–90 second audio demo (no talking, just sounds), a one-paragraph description written for a non-expert reader, and a Gumroad link. Make it easy for a blogger to copy-paste.

Follow up once, 2 weeks after sending. If no response after that, move on.

---

## 5. Podcast and YouTube Sponsorship

MPC-focused shows with 2K–10K listeners/viewers typically charge $50–150 per episode for a mid-roll mention. The math only works if the catalog can convert listeners: one sponsorship at $100 needs roughly 7–10 sales at $15 to break even, assuming some listeners find the pack later via search.

**Year 1 verdict**: Only worth testing after the catalog has 3+ packs. A single-pack sponsorship is too thin — listeners who don't want that specific pack have nowhere to go. With 3+ packs, there is a store to send people to. Prioritize organic discovery first.

---

## 6. Discord Server Partnerships

Beat-making Discord servers frequently have a `#resources` or `#free-samples` channel. A pinned free pack in 3–5 active servers is realistic and can drive 500–1,000 downloads with zero ad spend.

**Approach**: Join the server as a regular member first. Participate for a week or two. Then DM the admin directly — not a mod, the admin. Offer a free pack for the community, no strings. Ask if they'd be open to pinning it in the resources channel. Keep it casual. Servers that feel like they're being marketed to will ignore or reject the pitch; servers where you've already contributed will usually say yes.

Target servers: MPC community, Akai MPC official, Beat Academy, and any genre-specific production communities where MPC users are active.

---

## Year 1 Priority Order

1. Discord server drops (free, high reach, low effort)
2. Blog press kit outreach (free review copies, low cost)
3. Gumroad affiliate recruitment (20%, YouTubers and forum power users)
4. Cross-studio collab (one partner, one pack)
5. Podcast sponsorship (defer until 3+ packs)
6. Akai hardware bundle (Year 2+)
