# XPN Engine Roadmap 2026 — R&D Spec
**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Status**: Active Planning

---

## Overview

XOlokun ships with 34 registered engines — all seanced, all doctrine-compliant — plus 4 V1 concept engines pending DSP build (OSTINATO, OPENSKY, OCEANDEEP, OUIE). Expansion pack production must be sequenced to maximize momentum: early packs should have obvious MPC workflow fit, strong sonic identity, and broad producer appeal. Later packs can serve narrower audiences once the catalog has established trust.

This spec defines readiness tiers, production order, collection arcs, and coupling pack opportunities for the full 2026 calendar.

---

## 1. Pack Readiness Tiers

### Tier 1 — Ship Now (Q1–Q2 2026)
Engines with strong seance scores, clear sonic identity, and natural MPC workflow. These are the packs that sell themselves in a 30-second demo clip.

### Tier 2 — Ship Q2–Q3 2026
Engines with proven sound design potential but less obvious MPC ergonomics. These need more deliberate pack architecture — explicit keygroup layouts, program notes, or companion documentation.

### Tier 3 — Ship H2 2026
Engines pending DSP build. Pack production begins once engines clear auval and seance.

---

## 2. Tier 1 Engines — First 4–6 Packs

**ONSET** — The anchor. A full drum machine with 8 synthesis voices, 115+ factory presets, and 4 macro axes (MACHINE, PUNCH, SPACE, MUTATE). Every MPC user understands a drum pack immediately. Ships first, no debate.

**OBLONG** — Character bass with 72 source files, Apple Liquid Glass UI, PlaySurface chord/scale modes. Hip-hop and beat producers are the natural audience. Dense personality, easy to demo, immediate groove utility.

**OPAL** — Granular synthesis with 150 factory presets across 6 categories. Atmospheric producers — lo-fi, ambient, cinematic — are underserved in the MPC ecosystem. OPAL fills that gap with texture-forward sound design that rewards slow exploration.

**ORACLE** — GENDY stochastic engine. The experimental producer audience is small but passionate and vocal. ORACLE packs signal that XO_OX takes sound design seriously at the edges. One well-curated ORACLE pack earns more press coverage than three generic synth packs.

**OVERBITE** (BITE) — Five-macro bass character synth with 342 presets. The Gallery code and Fang White accent give this engine a distinct visual and sonic personality. Bass packs with strong character branding outperform generic bass packs; OVERBITE delivers that.

**OVERDUB** — Dub/reggae/delay performance FX synth. 38 parameters, 40 presets, 4 performance pads (FIRE, XOSEND, ECHO CUT, PANIC). The audience is specific — dub, reggae, sound system culture — but deeply loyal. A focused OVERDUB pack targeting that audience converts at high rates.

## 3. Tier 2 Engines — Q2–Q3 2026

**OVERWORLD** — NES/Genesis/SNES chip synth with 67 parameters, 8-voice poly, ERA crossfade, CRT UI, 15 color themes. Game audio producers are a growing MPC constituency. Needs pack architecture that showcases the ERA system explicitly — three sub-packs (NES, Genesis, SNES) within one release is a viable approach.

**ORGANON** — Variational metabolism engine. Modular and experimental producers. Pack design should lean into morphing and generative variation as a feature, not a curiosity.

**OUROBOROS** — Chaotic self-modulation. Noise and industrial producers. Pairs naturally with ORACLE (see Section 6). A standalone OUROBOROS pack works best as a limited, high-concept release — "The Feedback Bible" or similar.

**OBSIDIAN** — Crystal resonance, cinematic/ambient. Post-OPAL release; the ambient catalog should have breathing room between releases to avoid cannibalization.

**OCEANIC + OCELOT** — ShoreSystem pair. World music producers. Bundle as a single release — "The Shore Sessions" — to give the pairing context and justify the combined preset count.

**OSPREY + OSTERIA** — ShoreSystem pair, cultural percussion. Same logic as OCEANIC + OCELOT: bundle as a paired release. Percussion packs sell well when framed around cultural specificity rather than generic "world drums."

## 4. Tier 3 — Concept Engines Pending Build (H2 2026)

**OSTINATO** (communal drum circle) — Drums. Urgency is real: the MPC drum audience is the largest single segment. OSTINATO should move to DSP build immediately after V1 stabilizes. Target: seance complete by end of Q2, pack in Q3.

**OPENSKY** (euphoric shimmer, pure feliX pole) — Synth pads. Summer release timing is natural. Target: Q3 2026.

**OCEANDEEP** (abyssal bass, pure Oscar pole) — Bass. Natural pairing with ONSET for a drums+bass bundle. Target: Q3–Q4 2026.

**OUIE** (duophonic hammerhead, STRIFE↔LOVE axis) — Leads. Unique enough that it earns its own release context. Target: Q4 2026.

## 5. Collection Arc Recommendations

**Collection 1 — Kitchen Essentials (Q1–Q2 2026)**
6 engine packs released over 10 weeks, capped with a collection bundle at full price. The Kitchen Essentials architecture (6 quads × 4 engines, Voice × FX Recipe × Wildcard) provides natural grouping logic. Bundle discount of 30% drives conversion from individual buyers.

**Collection 2 — Travel/Water (Q2–Q3 2026)**
5 packs: Sail, Industrial, Leisure, Historical, Sable Island. Each pack maps to a genre-era combination. Release cadence: one pack every 3 weeks. Bundle at conclusion.

**Collection 3 — Artwork/Color (Q3–Q4 2026)**
5 packs from Color Quad A and B plus the Showmen and Aesthetes sets. The lore content (art history, color science) supports companion blog posts and potential zine release — drives organic reach beyond typical synth pack promotion.

---

## 6. Coupling Pack Opportunities

Three coupling packs exploit cross-engine signal routing for production workflows that neither engine delivers alone:

**ONSET + ORBITAL** — Drum machine feeding group envelope control. Target: beatmakers who want programmatic dynamics across a full kit. Release format: dual-engine preset bank with explicit routing documentation.

**OPAL + OVERDUB** — Granular texture into tape delay + spring reverb. The OPAL→DUB coupling is already doctrine-registered. This pack demonstrates it explicitly: granular clouds processed through the full OVERDUB signal chain. Target: atmospheric and dub producers simultaneously.

**ORACLE + OUROBOROS** — Stochastic generation feeding chaotic self-modulation. The most experimental pairing in the catalog. Release as a limited "Research Edition" — numbered, explicit about its experimental framing. Drives press coverage and positions XO_OX at the avant edge.

## Notes

- Seance quality scores are the primary gate for Tier 1 promotion. No engine moves to Tier 1 without a clean seance pass.
- Pack naming follows the pattern: Engine Name — Subtitle. Subtitles should be evocative, not descriptive.
- Audio demo clips (20 hero preset recordings per pack, see site-sample-recordings.md) are a hard dependency for any public release.
- Coupling packs require routing documentation in the pack notes — do not assume producers will discover the cross-engine signal path independently.
