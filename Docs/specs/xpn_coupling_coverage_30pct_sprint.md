## 3. Gap Analysis at 30%

Even after this sprint, several engines will remain undercoupled:

**Orphica** — external coverage limited to OPAL, ORACLE, OCEANIC. Needs pairings with OBLONG, OVERBITE, ONSET, OHM, FAT.

**Outwit** — external coverage limited to ORACLE, OUROBOROS, and a small Constellation seed. Needs pairings across 8+ engines; complexity of its 8-arm CA voice means coupling presets require careful parameter mapping.

**Overlap** — external coverage limited to OPAL, ORACLE, and sparse Constellation pairs. Needs systematic pairing with DRIFT, OBLONG, OVERBITE, ONSET.

**4 Concept Engines (OSTINATO, OPENSKY, OCEANDEEP, OUIE)** — zero coupling coverage possible until DSP is built and integrated. These 4 engines represent 4 × 30 = 120 potential pairs (against all other 30 engines), meaning the hard ceiling for coupling coverage without them is 441/561 = 78.6%. A true 100% pass is blocked until V1 DSP is complete.

## 5. Path to 50%

**50% target**: 281/561 pairs. From the projected 168 at 30%, that requires **113 more pairs**.

At the current wave rate of ~31 pairs per wave batch:
- **~4 additional waves** to reach 50% under ideal conditions
- Realistic estimate accounting for diminishing returns (harder pairs, concept engine blockage): **5–6 waves**

Systematic approach: rotate through each of the 27 built engines, generating 3 presets per uncovered pair in batches of 8–10 pairs per tool run. Priority order: anchor engines first (OBLONG, OVERBITE, OPAL, ORACLE), then mid-tier (ONSET, DRIFT, FAT), then Constellation five, then sparse engines (OUTWIT, OVERLAP, ORPHICA).

## 6. Quality vs. Quantity

Stub presets generated by automated coupling tools are musically functional but not curated. They establish timbral relationships without optimizing for feel, dynamics, or performance expression.

**Recommended quality pass trigger: 50% coverage (281 pairs).**

At that point, half the matrix is populated and patterns emerge — which pairings are expressive vs. mechanical, which stubs need velocity curve work, which need macro reassignment. A human curation pass at 50% is more efficient than incremental spot-fixes during generation.

Before the quality pass, tag all stub presets with `[STUB]` in the XPN preset name field so they are identifiable in MPC and can be batch-flagged for review.
