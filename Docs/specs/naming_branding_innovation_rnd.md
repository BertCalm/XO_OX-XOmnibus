## Foreword

The XO_OX naming problem is not a naming problem. It is a mythology problem.

The Sonic DNA system encodes six dimensions of character. All of this is real and complete and internally consistent.

And then a preset is named "Crystal_Drift."

That name is fine. It communicates something. But it does not do what the mythology can do. It does not locate the sound in the world. It does not tell you who discovered it or where. It does not use the vocabulary that distinguishes XO_OX from every other sound design company shipping MPC packs.

This document is the system for closing that gap. It covers five innovations in preset naming, four principles for pack visual identity, official pack names and taglines for all 40 unconventional kit concepts, and a depth-zone mythology integration system that makes every name feel located — like a coordinate on a map the producer learns to read over time.

This is not about being clever. It is about being consistent. The mythology is the product. The naming system is the mythology made navigable.

## Section 1: Preset Naming as Worldbuilding

### 1.1 Current Approach — Diagnosis

The existing convention (2–3 evocative words, max 30 chars, no jargon) works as a minimum viable naming system. Examples: `Ocelot_Winter_Hunt`, `Crystal_Drift`, `Gravity_Hollow`. The problems:

1. **No location.** The name floats. It could belong to any synthesizer company.
2. **No narrator.** Who found this? From the mythology — there is a researcher, an expedition, a water column to navigate.
3. **No system.** Producers cannot learn to predict what a name means. Each name is its own island.
4. **No polarity signal.** A name should tell you whether this is feliX territory or Oscar territory before you press play.

The five innovations below are not replacements — they are extensions. The existing convention becomes the fallback. The innovations become the primary vocabulary for packs that are designed with mythology intent from the start.

---

### Innovation 1: The Expedition Log Model

**Concept:** Name each preset as a field note from a scientific expedition into the XO_OX water column. The expedition is continuous. Every preset is a discovery logged by the researcher (the sound designer) exploring the aquatic world. The preset list becomes a journal.

**Format options:**

```
Day 47 — Bioluminescent Corridor
Depth: 3200m — Thermal Vent Discovery
Specimen #0091 — Ink Cloud Defense
Station 7 — Pressure Anomaly
Log 0134 — Colonial Organism Contact
Survey Grid C7 — Magnetic Drift
```

**Rules:**
- "Day" entries: surface and twilight zone discoveries (0–1000m). Fast, recent, energetic.
- "Depth:" entries: specific meter readings for abyssal and hadal zone discoveries (1000m+). Dark, slow, weighted.
- "Specimen #" entries: OPAL and OUTWIT (granular/CA engines specifically) — catalogued lifeforms with behavioral descriptions.
- "Station" entries: ONSET and ORBITAL — fixed monitoring points, repeatable measurements.
- "Log" entries: ORACLE and ORGANON — research journal entries with subjective voice allowed.

**Concrete translation examples:**

| Old name | Expedition Log name |
|----------|-------------------|
| `Blue_Glow_Bass` | `Depth: 800m — Bioluminescent Column` |
| `Dark_Sub_Growl` | `Depth: 3200m — Thermal Vent Contact` |
| `Wide_Reverb_Pad` | `Specimen #0091 — Ink Cloud Defense` |
| `Fast_Pluck_Lead` | `Day 12 — Mantis Strike Sequence` |
| `Slow_Choir_Pad` | `Log 0047 — Colonial Resonance Field` |

**Why this works:** The expedition log creates temporal continuity across a preset bank. When a producer opens a pack of 150 OPAL presets and sees Day 1 through Day 150, they understand they are reading a story. The sound design becomes archaeological. The mythology is not decorative — it is structural.

### Innovation 2: Coordinate Naming

**Concept:** Preset names encode their Sonic DNA as geographic coordinates. Producers learn to navigate by reading coordinates the way a sailor reads charts.

**The coordinate grammar:**

Sonic DNA has six dimensions: brightness (B), warmth (W), movement (M), density (D), space (S), aggression (A). Each runs 0.0–1.0.

A coordinate name selects two dominant dimensions and encodes them as a sector address:

```
B9·A2    = Brightness 0.9 + Aggression 0.2  = high-bright, low-aggression (crystalline lead)
W7·M8    = Warmth 0.7 + Movement 0.8        = warm, fast (organic rhythm pad)
A9·D9    = Aggression 0.9 + Density 0.9     = heavy, dense (wall-of-sound)
S8·B1    = Space 0.8 + Brightness 0.1       = dark, wide (deep reverb wash)
```

**Paired-engine sectors:**

When presets use two coupled engines, the sector name combines their identities:

```
ONSET × OPAL     = Sector PERC-OPAL     (percussive granular space)
OVERDUB × OPAL   = Sector DUB-OPAL      (tape delay granular)
ORACLE × OUTWIT  = Sector ORACLE-CA     (stochastic + cellular automaton)
OVERWORLD × OHM  = Sector ERA-COMMUNE   (chip synth + harmonic drone)
```

**Usage guidance:** Coordinate names are most powerful in advanced packs and coupled-engine collections where producers are already familiar with the Sonic DNA system. Use expedition log names for introductory packs. Graduate producers to coordinate names as they deepen their XO_OX fluency.

**Tag-team application:** A preset can carry BOTH a log name and a coordinate tag:

```
Day 47 — Bioluminescent Corridor   [B8·S9]
```

The log name is poetic. The coordinate is functional. Together they are complete.

### Innovation 3: Depth Zone Territory Naming

**Concept:** The water column depth zones (from the XO_OX aquatic mythology) function as naming territories. Each zone has an approved vocabulary, emotional register, and sentence rhythm. Producers learn: "I need something darker — go to Trench names."

**Zone vocabulary tables:**

**Sunlit Zone (0–200m)** — Surface tier
- Emotional register: fast, bright, alive, social
- Approved nouns: current, surface, bloom, shoal, reef, light, wave, breath, canopy, chase
- Approved verbs: breaks, darts, spirals, flickers, erupts, cascades
- Name rhythm: short + active. Two nouns or noun + verb.
- Examples: `Surface Break`, `Reef Canopy`, `Light Dart`, `Shoal Chase`
- Engines native to this zone: ODDFELIX, OCELOT, ORPHICA, OPENSKY (concept)

**Twilight Zone (200–1000m)** — Transition tier
- Emotional register: transitional, searching, between-worlds, dimming
- Approved nouns: boundary, gradient, descent, pressure, silhouette, threshold, fade, drift
- Approved verbs: sinks, fades, crosses, filters, diffuses
- Name rhythm: noun + state, or adjective + noun suggesting transformation
- Examples: `Gradient Descent`, `Pressure Threshold`, `Silhouette Fade`, `Boundary Crossing`
- Engines native to this zone: OPAL, OVERDUB, OBLIQUE, OMBRE

**Midnight Zone (1000–3000m)** — Alien tier
- Emotional register: alien, unexpected, bioluminescent, pressure-warped
- Approved nouns: biolume, vestige, phantom, flash, colony, chimera, filament, void-bloom
- Approved verbs: pulses, haunts, colonizes, flares, dissolves
- Name rhythm: unusual compound nouns, unexpected combinations. The name should feel slightly wrong and exactly right.
- Examples: `Void Bloom`, `Phantom Colony`, `Biolume Flash`, `Chimera Filament`
- Engines native to this zone: ORACLE, ORGANON, OUTWIT, OCEANIC

**Abyssal Zone (3000–6000m)** — Geological tier
- Emotional register: ancient, slow, sedimentary, massive, patient
- Approved nouns: sediment, vent, ridge, pressure-field, fossil, relic, strata, mass
- Approved verbs: accumulates, settles, pressurizes, fossilizes, forms
- Name rhythm: geological + patient. Long vowels preferred.
- Examples: `Sediment Ridge`, `Abyssal Relic`, `Vent Accumulation`, `Pressure Strata`
- Engines native to this zone: OBSIDIAN, OUROBOROS, ORCA, OCEANDEEP (concept)

**Hadal Zone (6000m+)** — Extinction tier
- Emotional register: extinction-level, irreversible, absolute, final
- Approved nouns: trench, terminus, crush-depth, abyss, event, silence, convergence
- Approved verbs: converges, terminates, crushes, erases, collapses
- Name rhythm: terminal and absolute. Names should feel like final entries.
- Examples: `Trench Terminus`, `Crush Depth Event`, `Hadal Convergence`, `Silence Below`
- Engines native to this zone: ONSET (at max aggression), OBESE, OSTINATO (concept)

**Sky tier (above surface)** — Release tier
- Emotional register: height, freedom, release, escape from gravity
- Approved nouns: atmosphere, altitude, stratosphere, escape, apex, horizon, launch
- Approved verbs: ascends, releases, clears, launches, thins
- Name rhythm: upward motion implied. Final words should feel like arrival.
- Examples: `Apex Release`, `Stratosphere Clear`, `Altitude Horizon`, `Escape Velocity`
- Engines native to this zone: OVERWORLD, OSPREY, ODDFELIX (bright modes)

### Innovation 4: Character Voice Naming

**Concept:** Every engine has a biological creature identity. Names written in the creature's "voice" carry the engine's character at the naming layer — before the producer even opens the preset.

**Per-engine voice guides:**

**ONSET — Mantis Shrimp**
The mantis shrimp strikes at 23 m/s, generating cavitation bubbles at 46,000+ g acceleration. Fast, violent, precise, repetitive. No wasted words.
- Voice: short, mechanical, clinical. Names read like technical specifications.
- Examples: `Strike Sequence 1`, `Foreleg Extension`, `Dactyl Club Impact`, `Cavitation Event`, `Raptorial Strike`, `Thermal Bloom`
- Anti-examples (wrong register): `Warm Groove`, `Smooth Pad` — ONSET does not do gentle

**OPAL — Sea Angel (Clione limacina)**
Translucent, slow, drifting. A pelagic predator that looks like nothing that hunts. It dissolves prey from the inside.
- Voice: patient, granular, patient. Time runs differently here.
- Examples: `Dissolution State`, `Pellucid Drift`, `Frozen Wings`, `Slow Emergence`, `Transparent Body`, `Parapodia Extension`

**ORACLE — Anglerfish (Melanocetus johnsonii)**
Deep water, total darkness, a single bioluminescent lure. Everything is probability here. The light may or may not attract prey. The algorithm dreams.
- Voice: prophetic, uncertain, incomplete. Names suggest incompleteness or prophecy.
- Examples: `Prophecy Fragment`, `When the Algorithm Dreams`, `Lure Activation`, `Void Contact`, `Stochastic Depth`, `Signal from Nowhere`

**ORGANON — Portuguese Man o' War (Physalia physalis)**
Not an organism — a colony of zooids, each with a specific metabolic role. What looks like a single creature is distributed intelligence running parallel biological processes.
- Voice: biological, process-oriented, systems language.
- Examples: `ATP Synthesis State`, `Colony Resonance`, `Metabolic Phase 3`, `Distributed Signal`, `Pneumatophore Ascent`, `Polyp Activation`

**OUTWIT — Giant Pacific Octopus (Enteroctopus dofleini)**
8 arms, each with 280 suckers, each sucker semi-autonomous. Wolfram cellular automaton = 8 arms running parallel rule sets.
- Voice: plural, distributed, each arm slightly different. Names suggest multiplicity.
- Examples: `Arm 4 Activation`, `Eight-Arm Cascade`, `Chromatophore State 7`, `Sucker Array`, `Ink Cloud Defense`, `Mantle Expansion`

**ORACLE additional note:** ORACLE presets can use the incomplete sentence as a naming device — the preset name itself is stochastic, like the engine:
- `If the light—`
- `Sequence: unknown`
- `[signal lost]`
These should be used sparingly (max 10% of an ORACLE pack) to create rhythm contrast.

### Innovation 5: The Anti-Preset System

**Concept:** For every "hero" preset (beautiful, consonant, functional), design an "anti-preset" — the same source parameters but with the feliX-Oscar polarity inverted, all modulation reversed, envelope stages swapped, and harmonic emphasis mirrored.

Anti-presets are frequently more interesting than the originals. They are also a natural pair-selling mechanism: the hero preset and the villain preset ship together as a duality.

**Anti-preset naming convention:**

```
Crystal Drift          → Anti-Crystal              (polarity inverted)
Day 47 Bioluminescent  → Night 47 — Ink Void        (light inverted to dark)
Shoal Chase            → Trench Stall               (direction + depth inverted)
Strike Sequence 1      → Dissolution State 1        (ONSET → OPAL)
```

**The Villain Pack concept:**

A dedicated pack containing ONLY anti-presets, marketed as the evil mirror of the hero library. Every sound in the Villain Pack corresponds to a named sound in the flagship pack. The Villain Pack is not the "dark version" — it is the structural inverse. This is a product differentiation strategy no other MPC pack company has attempted because no other company has a mythology in which good and evil are structural poles (feliX and Oscar) built into the synthesis architecture.

**Pack marketing language:** "Every hero has a shadow. These are the shadows."

## Section 2: Pack Identity Systems

### 2.1 The Visual Grammar

Each XO_OX pack requires five visual grammar decisions made consistently:

**1. Creature stamp**
The engine's creature silhouette as a geometric stamp — not a realistic illustration, but a high-contrast shape that reads at thumbnail size. The mantis shrimp for ONSET. The sea angel for OPAL. The anglerfish for ORACLE. This stamp appears in the pack cover corner, the MPC Store thumbnail, and the DNA badge center.

**2. Color temperature encoding**
feliX packs (high brightness, low aggression) use cool color temperatures: blues, greens, silvers. Oscar packs (low brightness, high aggression) use warm color temperatures: reds, ambers, deep purples.

Cross-polarity coupled packs use a split: feliX on the left, Oscar on the right, gradient through the engine accent color in center.

**3. Typography style by sonic weight**
- Heavy percussion (ONSET, OBESE, ORCA, OVERWORLD heavy modes): block sans-serif, all caps, tight tracking
- Melodic / pitched (OPAL, OVERDUB, ODYSSEY, OBBLIGATO): light weight, mixed case, generous leading
- Hybrid / coupled: alternating weights between the two contributing engine typographic registers

**4. Pattern language**
Engine accent color as a structural pattern element — not background decoration, but a data-bearing visual:
- ONSET (Electric Blue `#0066FF`): vertical strike marks, dense spacing, varying heights = strike sequences
- OPAL (Lavender `#A78BFA`): soft concentric ellipses, fading outward = granular scatter
- ORACLE (Prophecy Indigo `#4B0082`): interrupted lines, dots not connected = stochastic signal
- ORGANON (Bioluminescent Cyan `#00CED1`): branching diagrams = metabolic pathway maps
- OUTWIT (Orange `#CC6600`): 8-spoke radial patterns = octopus arms

**5. Depth zone band**
Every pack cover includes a thin horizontal band (bottom 8% of image) in the depth zone color. This creates visual continuity across the entire catalog — a producer scanning their MPC pack library can read depth from the band colors alone.

| Zone | Band color |
|------|-----------|
| Hadal (6000m+) | Near-black with slight violet tint `#0D0008` |
| Abyssal (3000–6000m) | Deep void blue `#050A1A` |
| Midnight (1000–3000m) | Dark teal-black `#031A19` |
| Twilight (200–1000m) | Dim indigo `#1A1535` |
| Sunlit (0–200m) | Rich ocean blue `#0047AB` |
| Surface / Sky | Luminous cyan-white `#C0E8F0` |

### 2.2 Pack Naming Conventions

**Official XO_OX pack naming system:**

| Pack type | Format | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Solo engine, volume | `{ENGINE} Vol{N}` | `ONSET Vol1` |
| Coupled pair | `{ENGINE1}×{ENGINE2}` | `ONSET×OPAL` |
| Collection set | `{COLLECTION} — {SET}` | `KITCHEN — Fire` |
| Concept pack | `{CONCEPT} / {ENGINE}` | `Braille / ONSET` |
| Musician identity | `{MUSICIAN} × {ENGINE}` | `JDilla × OPAL` |
| Anti/villain | `{ENGINE} — Shadowbank` | `ONSET — Shadowbank` |
| Mythology deep-dive | `{DEPTH_ZONE} — {ENGINE}` | `Hadal — ORCA` |
| Cross-polarity | `feliX→Oscar / {COUPLED}` | `feliX→Oscar / OVERDUB×ONSET` |

**The × symbol** (Unicode U+00D7, multiplication sign) is the XO_OX coupling symbol. It signals that two engines are co-present and interacting. This is different from a collaboration (×) vs. a collection set (—) which contains multiple engines in sequence.

### 2.3 The DNA Badge System

Every pack gets a hexagonal DNA badge — a six-segment hexagon, each segment shaded in proportion to that Sonic DNA dimension's median value across all presets in the pack.

**Segment positions (clockwise from 12 o'clock):**
1. **Brightness** (12 o'clock / top) — shaded from `#F8F6F3` (0.0) to `#FFEE00` (1.0)
2. **Warmth** (2 o'clock) — shaded from `#E8F4FF` (0.0) to `#FF6B00` (1.0)
3. **Movement** (4 o'clock) — shaded from `#F0F0F0` (0.0) to `#00CED1` (1.0)
4. **Density** (6 o'clock / bottom) — shaded from `#F8F6F3` (0.0) to `#1A1A2E` (1.0)
5. **Space** (8 o'clock) — shaded from `#F0F0F0` (0.0) to `#4B0082` (1.0)
6. **Aggression** (10 o'clock) — shaded from `#F8F6F3` (0.0) to `#FF2D2D` (1.0)

**Badge shapes by pack type:**
- Solid hexagon outline: solo engine pack
- Double hexagon (nested): coupled pair pack
- Hexagon with creature stamp center: flagship/collection pack

The badge is 120×120px for MPC Store thumbnails. The outer hexagon is `#E9C46A` (XO Gold). Producers learn to read badges in 3–5 hours of using XO_OX packs. After that, the badge communicates a pack's entire character at a glance — faster than reading 150 preset names.

## Section 4: Mythology Integration — The Complete System

### 4.1 The Depth Zone → Pack Theme Matrix

Every XO_OX pack should be locatable in depth. This is not decoration — it is information. A producer browsing the MPC Store should be able to look at a pack name and depth band color and know before previewing: how heavy is this? How old is this? How alien is this?

**Hadal Zone (6000m+) — Extinction-Level Packs**

Character: dark, heavy, irreversible, final, absolute pressure. Nothing from the hadal zone returns unchanged. Sounds here have been compressed and distorted by forces that cannot be simulated — only approximated.

Hadal packs feel like endings. Like something that happened a long time ago and cannot be undone. The sub-bass is geological. The transients are sparse because at this depth, motion is expensive. Silence is not rest — it is the weight of six kilometers of water above.

Approved engines for hadal packs: ORCA, OBSIDIAN, OBESE (extreme modes), ONSET (maximum aggression), OCEANDEEP (concept), OUROBOROS (convergence states).

Hadal naming rule: use terminal language. Names should feel like final log entries. "Hadal Convergence." "Crush Depth Event." "Trench Terminus." Nothing hopeful. Nothing that implies return.

**Abyssal Zone (3000–6000m) — Ancient Packs**

Character: geological time, sedimentation, deep pressure, slow movement. Abyssal sounds are patient because they have no other option. The temperature is 2°C. The pressure is 300–600 atmospheres. Nothing here is fast.

Abyssal packs feel like memory. Like sounds that have existed so long they've become features of the landscape. The low end is massive but not aggressive — it is simply present, the way bedrock is present. The decay times are measured in seconds, not milliseconds.

Approved engines: OBSIDIAN, OVERDUB (slow settings), ODYSSEY (deep patch modes), OUROBOROS, ORGANON (slow metabolic states).

Abyssal naming rule: geological and sedimentary. "Sediment Ridge." "Thermal Vent Contact." "Abyssal Relic." Time is measured in millennia here.

**Midnight Zone (1000–3000m) — Alien Packs**

Character: bioluminescent, unexpected, between-worlds, alien biology, extreme specialization. Life here is strange by evolutionary necessity. Bioluminescence is a language. Communication happens through light pulses in total darkness.

Midnight packs feel genuinely alien — not science-fiction alien, but real-ocean alien. The sounds are unexpected because the organisms that produced them are unprecedented. OPAL, ORACLE, and OUTWIT live here. So does OCEANIC. The granular synthesis, the stochastic GENDY, the cellular automaton arms — these are midnight-zone technologies.

Midnight naming rule: compound nouns that feel slightly wrong. The name should not quite parse on first read, then snap into focus on second. "Void Bloom." "Phantom Colony." "Chimera Filament." "Biolume Flash." These are things you would see on a research submersible camera at 2000m and not have words for.

**Twilight Zone (200–1000m) — Transitional Packs**

Character: between-worlds, the diel vertical migration highway, the boundary layer between abundant sunlight and total darkness. Creatures here commute daily between the surface and the midnight zone. Everything here is in motion between states.

Twilight packs feel like transitions. Like sounds that are becoming something else. The coupled packs — OVERDUB×OPAL, OMBRE×ODYSSEY, OBLIQUE×ORACLE — live here. They are neither one engine nor the other. They are the gradient.

Twilight naming rule: transformation language, descent/ascent implied. "Gradient Descent." "Pressure Threshold." "Boundary Crossing." "Silhouette Fade." The sound is on its way somewhere.

**Sunlit Zone (0–200m) — Surface Packs**

Character: fast, bright, alive, social, abundant. This is where 90% of life in the ocean lives. The reef. The shoal. The mantis shrimp's strike. The sea angel's drift through open water.

Sunlit packs are energetic. They are for producers who are in motion — making beats fast, trying sounds fast, discarding fast, keeping the ones that work. ONSET Vol1 is a sunlit pack. ODDFELIX and OCELOT live here. ORPHICA's plucked microsound lives here. These sounds feel like things that want to be heard.

Sunlit naming rule: short, active, sensory. "Reef Canopy." "Light Dart." "Surface Break." "Shoal Chase." The name should feel like something you would do, not something that would be done to you.

**Sky Tier (above surface) — Release Packs**

Character: altitude, freedom, release from gravity, the moment of escape. OVERWORLD is the sky-tier engine by design — chip sounds derived from NES/Genesis/SNES, the digital sound of the early internet age, which was a form of escape from physical space.

Sky packs feel like endings that are also beginnings. Like sounds that have shed weight. OPENSKY (concept) belongs here. OSPREY's coastal flight patterns. The top of ODDFELIX's brightness range.

Sky naming rule: ascent, altitude, escape, horizon. "Apex Release." "Stratosphere Clear." "Altitude Horizon." "Escape Velocity." The name should make you want to be somewhere higher than you are.

### 4.2 Creature → Pack Character

The biological creature associated with each engine is not aesthetic decoration. It is a behavioral specification. What a creature DOES is what the engine DOES. The pack character follows from the creature character.

**Mantis Shrimp → ONSET**
Strike speed: 23 m/s. Acceleration: 46,000g. The fastest limb movement of any animal on Earth. The strike creates cavitation bubbles that collapse at 4,600°C. The mantis shrimp is a weapon that happens to also be an animal.

ONSET packs are mantis shrimp packs. They are fast, violent, precise, and technically astounding. The sound design should feel mechanically perfect. There is no warmth here. There is only accuracy and force. Pack names should honor the biology: "Strike Sequence," "Dactyl Club Impact," "Cavitation Event." The producer should feel like they are deploying something engineered rather than played.

**Sea Angel → OPAL**
Clione limacina. Translucent pelagic predator. Wings that are actually feet. Body that is 99% water. Dissolves prey from the inside. Drifts rather than swims, moves with current rather than against it.

OPAL packs are sea angel packs. The granular synthesis is the translucent body — light passes through, nothing is opaque. The patience is the drift. The predation is the dissolution — sounds that you don't notice entering a mix until they have changed everything. "Dissolution State." "Pellucid Drift." "Transparent Body." The danger in OPAL is the danger of being dissolved without noticing.

**Anglerfish → ORACLE**
Total darkness. Single bioluminescent lure. The lure attracts. What follows is not what was expected. The GENDY stochastic synthesis mirrors this: the algorithm generates something that sounds like music, but the rules that generated it are opaque. The lure is the interesting sound. The trap is the probability density function beneath.

ORACLE packs are anglerfish packs. The sound is attractive. The behavior is uncertain. Pack names suggest bait, incompletion, prophecy. "When the Algorithm Dreams." "Lure Activation." "Signal from Nowhere." "Prophecy Fragment." The producer should feel like something is being offered that may or may not be trustworthy.

**Portuguese Man o' War → ORGANON**
Not one organism. A colonial siphonophore — four types of zooids (pneumatophores, nectophores, dactylozooids, gonozooids) each with a specialized metabolic function. The colony is the organism. Intelligence is distributed.

ORGANON packs are colonial packs. The metabolic synthesis architecture mirrors the zooid specialization. Pack names use systems biology vocabulary: "Colony Resonance," "Metabolic Phase 3," "Distributed Signal," "ATP Synthesis State." The producer should feel like they are observing biological processes, not performing them.

**Giant Pacific Octopus → OUTWIT**
Eight arms. Each arm has 280 suckers. Each sucker is semi-autonomous with its own nervous system. The octopus processes 2/3 of its neural computation in the arms, not the brain. Wolfram cellular automaton = eight arms running parallel rule sets, each arm's output feeding back as another arm's input.

OUTWIT packs are octopus packs. The eight-arm Wolfram CA architecture is the sucker-arm semi-autonomy. Pack names should be plural and distributed: "Arm 4 Activation," "Eight-Arm Cascade," "Chromatophore State 7." The producer should feel like they are coordinating multiple semi-independent agents rather than playing a single instrument.

**Humpback Whale → OCEANIC**
Long-form, 20Hz–24kHz range, song structures lasting 30 minutes per cycle. The whale is not in a hurry. It is the CHROMATOPHORE engine modulation transferred upward to song-scale time. OCEANIC packs are long. They are melodic even in their bass lines. They are for producers who want to compose, not produce.

OCEANIC packs: "Song Fragment — 3 Minutes." "Whale Road." "Thermal Layer Transmission." Pack names should respect scale — these sounds travel thousands of miles in the real ocean.

**Mimic Octopus → OMBRE / dual-narrative engines**
Thaumoctopus mimicus. Can impersonate 15+ species: flatfish, lionfish, sea snake. Genre-defiant by nature. No fixed identity.

OMBRE packs are mimic octopus packs. The dual-narrative architecture (memory/forgetting + perception) is the impersonation capability. Pack names suggest instability of identity: "The Flatfish Pretense." "Lionfish State." "Who the Mirror Thinks You Are." The producer should feel like the instrument itself is not sure what it is.

**Apex Predator → ORCA**
Orcinus orca. Not a dolphin. A dolphin is an orca that gave up. The orca hunts everything — great white sharks, blue whales, humpback calves. Echolocation precision to within 1mm. Pack-hunting intelligence that passes strategies between generations as cultural knowledge.

ORCA packs are apex-predator packs. No ambiguity, no warmth, no decorative elements. Pack names: "Hunt Sequence," "Echolocation Lock," "Pack Strategy," "Breach Event." The producer should feel like they have access to something that does not negotiate.

### 4.3 The feliX-Oscar Depth Axis

The feliX-Oscar polarity aligns with depth. This is not accidental — it is structural.

feliX (the neon tetra) lives near the surface. Bright, fast, colorful, social, visible. Oscar (the axolotl) lives at the bottom of brackish water in a cave system. Slow, patient, regenerative, dark, alone.

**feliX engines are surface engines.** Their natural packs live in the sunlit and sky tiers. Their names use sunlit vocabulary. Their colors are cool and bright.

**Oscar engines are depth engines.** Their natural packs live in the twilight, midnight, and abyssal tiers. Their names use depth vocabulary. Their colors are warm and dark.

**Coupling = descent.** When a feliX engine couples with an Oscar engine, the pack is always in the twilight zone — the boundary layer where sunlit and midnight world organisms meet. The coupled pack name should acknowledge both registers: sunlit vocabulary on the left, depth vocabulary on the right, joined by the × coupling symbol.

Example: `Surface Break × Pressure Threshold` for ODDFELIX × OBSIDIAN.

This axis is the deepest naming principle. When in doubt: ask which engine is feliX-dominant and which is Oscar-dominant. Assign depth accordingly. Name from depth vocabulary. The rest follows.

### 4.4 Practical Application: Naming Checklist

For any new preset or pack:

1. **Identify the engine's creature.** What does that creature do?
2. **Identify the depth zone.** Is this sunlit, twilight, midnight, abyssal, or hadal?
3. **Identify the feliX-Oscar polarity.** Which pole dominates?
4. **Select the naming innovation.** Expedition log? Coordinate? Character voice? Zone vocabulary?
5. **Draft the name.** Max 30 chars for presets. Full phrase for pack titles.
6. **Apply the zone vocabulary filter.** Is the name using the approved vocabulary for its zone?
7. **Check creature voice.** Does the name sound like something the creature would generate?
8. **Verify polarity signal.** Can a producer guess feliX or Oscar before pressing play?

If all eight pass: the name is located. It belongs to the world. Use it.
