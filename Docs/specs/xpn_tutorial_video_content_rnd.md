# XPN Tutorial Video Content — R&D Spec
**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Status**: Planning

---

## 1. Video Content Philosophy

Tutorials are trust-building, not feature demonstrations. The goal is not to show producers how to navigate menus — it is to answer the question they are actually asking: **why does this sound better?**

Every XO_OX tutorial starts with the result. Play the beat, play the preset, let the sound land before explaining anything. Producers on MPC are listening first. If the sound doesn't hook them in the first ten seconds, the technique doesn't matter.

This means leading with the sound, not the interface. Show the patch playing in a loop before cutting to the screen. Show the finished beat before explaining how the ONSET kick was tuned. The explanation earns its place after the listener is already curious.

XO_OX tutorials are for MPC producers, not synthesizer nerds. Avoid oscillator topology language, filter pole counts, and modulation matrix jargon unless you immediately follow it with a plain-English translation and a before/after audio demo. The audience knows what sounds good. They want to know how to get there faster.

---

## 2. Video Types and Formats

**Type A — Pack Walkthrough** (3–5 min, YouTube primary)
Plays through 5 programs from a single pack in a beat context. Not a patch-by-patch menu tour — a mini-session showing what the pack sounds like in use. Each program gets 20–30 seconds of musical context: a loop, a stab, a texture. Closes with a full-beat demo using all 5 programs together. This is the primary sales video for each pack.

**Type B — Engine Deep Dive** (8–12 min, YouTube primary)
Takes one XOlokun engine and explores its sound design philosophy using a specific pack as the example material. Not a parameter walkthrough. Answers: what is this engine's character, what does it do that other engines don't, and how do the pack presets reflect that? ONSET deep dive covers why MPC drum machines have a physical quality. OPAL deep dive covers why granular works differently than sampling. Requires more prep but builds the most durable audience trust.

**Type C — Quick Tip** (60–90 sec vertical, Instagram/TikTok primary)
One technique or workflow insight, no more. Hook in the first 3 seconds, deliver the tip, show the result, done. Repurposed from longer YouTube content where possible — not produced from scratch every time. These are the discovery engine. Most new viewers will find XO_OX through a Quick Tip first.

**Type D — Behind the Pack** (15–20 min, Patreon exclusive)
Full pack design session from concept to final program. Shows the process: what sounds were rejected, what decisions were made and why, how a pack theme shapes preset design. This is the highest-value exclusive for Patreon members — raw, extended, behind-the-curtain access that YouTube content deliberately doesn't provide.

## 3. Production Priority — First 6 Videos

1. **TIDE TABLES pack walkthrough** — Type A. Anchor video for the first free pack launch. Establishes the format and sets audience expectations for all future walkthroughs.

2. **Quick tip: MPCe quad corners explained in 60 seconds** — Type C. High-search-intent topic, answers a question new MPCe owners are actively asking. Fast to produce, high discovery potential.

3. **ONSET engine deep dive: why MPC drum machines sound good** — Type B. ONSET is the most immediately legible engine for MPC producers. A well-made deep dive here builds credibility for the weirder engines later.

4. **Quick tip: coupling on MPC — how to use Entangled presets** — Type C. Addresses the one concept most likely to confuse new XOlokun users. Reduces friction before it becomes a support question.

5. **Behind the Pack: MACHINE GUN REEF design session** — Type D, Patreon exclusive. Rewards early Patreon members with access to the full creative process behind a marquee pack.

6. **CHROMODORIS DRIFT walkthrough** — Type A. Covers the OPAL free pack and introduces the granular engine to an audience that may not know what granular synthesis means for their sound.

## 4. Platform Strategy

YouTube is the long-form home. All Type A and Type B videos live here first. SEO titles target "MPC expansion pack" plus the engine name — "ONSET engine deep dive MPC drum sounds," "OPAL granular MPC expansion." Descriptions include pack download link, Patreon link, and at least three timestamps.

Instagram Reels and TikTok carry the Type C content. Quick Tips are filmed with vertical framing in mind from the start, not cropped after the fact. TikTok has the highest ceiling for discovery — a single Quick Tip on MPCe quad corners could reach producers who have never heard of XO_OX.

Patreon gets Type D exclusively. No Type D content is ever posted publicly. The exclusivity is the value.

---

## 5. Audio Quality Requirements

MPC producer audiences have calibrated ears. Audio quality is a trust signal before a single word is spoken.

Record in a treated room or with acoustic panels behind the mic. Show MPC hardware on camera — the physical unit communicates authenticity in a way screen recordings alone do not. Use OBS or equivalent for screen-plus-camera composite. Export at 4K even if the shoot is 1080p; YouTube's algorithm treats 4K uploads differently and the headroom costs nothing in post.

---

## 6. SEO and Discovery Strategy

Primary keyword targets: "MPC expansion packs," "MPC Live III sounds," "Akai MPC tutorial," "MPCe pads." Secondary targets built around engine names as they build recognition.

The funnel runs: YouTube/TikTok discovery → XO-OX.org → Gumroad purchase or Patreon signup. Every video description closes with the same three links in the same order: pack download, full pack catalog, Patreon.

Cross-link aggressively between videos. The ONSET deep dive links to the TIDE TABLES walkthrough. The CHROMODORIS walkthrough links to the OPAL Quick Tip. Build the web early.

---

## 7. Production Cadence

**Q2 2026**: 2 Type A + 4 Type C per month. This is sustainable with current resources and builds the catalog without burning out production.

**Starting Q3 2026**: Add 1 Type B per month and 1 Type D per month on Patreon. By this point the format is established, the audience knows what to expect, and the deeper content has earned its place.

Batch Type C filming where possible — four Quick Tips in one session, published across the month. Type A walkthroughs are tied to pack release timing and cannot be batched the same way. Type B and D require dedicated prep and should not be rushed.
