# XPN Customer Journey Map — R&D Spec
**Date:** 2026-03-16
**Project:** XO_OX Pack Ecosystem

---

## Purpose

This spec maps the six-stage journey from first exposure to vocal advocate. Each stage identifies what the producer is thinking, what they need, what XO_OX provides, and the specific trigger that moves them forward. Use this to audit touchpoints, identify gaps, and prioritize build work.

---

## Stage 1: Discovery

**What they're thinking:** Nothing about XO_OX — they're scrolling, making beats, looking for sounds.

**Trigger:** A 15-second Sound Post on TikTok, a Reddit comment linking to the site, a YouTube video where a beat maker drops a preset name.

**What they need:** To hear something that makes them stop. Not a patch demo — a beat in context. The sound has to feel immediate and usable.

**What XO_OX provides:** Sound Posts — short clips built around a single preset, beat-in-context, showing the sound doing work in a groove. Cover art that signals a coherent visual identity, not a freelancer pack thumbnail.

**What moves them forward:** They save the post, click a link in bio, or search "XO_OX" on their own. The social handle and domain must be consistent so search works.

**Metric:** Link clicks or profile visits per Sound Post.

---

## Stage 2: Curiosity

**What they're thinking:** "What is this, is it for me, can I trust it?"

**What they need:** Fast orientation — what kind of sounds, what format, what does it cost, is there a free entry point.

**What XO_OX provides:** TIDE TABLES free pack available on the landing page — immediate download, no email gate. The Field Guide article on sonic philosophy gives context without requiring purchase. Cover art and site design signal premium positioning without a premium price barrier.

**What moves them forward:** They download the free pack. The download should take fewer than three clicks from landing page to file in hand.

**Metric:** Free pack download rate from landing page visits.

---

## Stage 3: First Experience

**What they're thinking:** "Let me see if these sounds are actually usable."

**What they need:** An immediate win — one sound that drops into a beat in under five minutes. If they have to fight the file structure or figure out MPC loading on their own, they quit.

**What XO_OX provides:** TIDE TABLES designed for immediate usability (labeled patches, logical pad layout, sensible velocities). MPCE_SETUP.md bundled in the pack — not linked externally, bundled. Pack walkthrough video linked from the README showing exactly how to load and browse.

**What moves them forward:** They make something — even eight bars — using a sound from the pack. That moment of "this sounds good in my beat" is the conversion point.

**Metric:** Return visit to XO-OX.org within 7 days of download.

---

## Stage 4: Investment

**What they're thinking:** "I want more of this — is MACHINE GUN REEF worth $15?"

**What they need:** Evidence that a paid pack is meaningfully different from the free tier. Not just "more sounds" — a clear articulation of what FORM tier depth means in practice.

**What XO_OX provides:** Pack preview card showing the DNA breakdown (synthesis engines used, mood categories, patch count). A short comparison note explaining TIDE TABLES vs MACHINE GUN REEF in terms of complexity and playability. Patreon early access teaser as a secondary hook — signals ongoing output, not a one-time download.

**What moves them forward:** They buy. The $15 price point is calibrated against the free pack anchoring — TIDE TABLES makes $15 feel like a reasonable step, not a gamble.

**Metric:** Free-to-paid conversion rate (downloads that result in a purchase within 30 days).

---

## Stage 5: Commitment

**What they're thinking:** "I keep coming back to these packs — is there more to this?"

**What they need:** To feel like part of something with a direction, not just a customer buying product. Access to the process, not just the output.

**What XO_OX provides:** Patreon tiers — even the $5 tier signals membership. Behind-the-scenes posts on collection direction. Community Q&A. Voting on next collection theme. Field Guide articles that reward ongoing engagement by going deeper on the mythology and DSP decisions.

**What moves them forward:** They join Patreon. The $5 tier must deliver something tangible on day one — early download, exclusive preview, not just "support the project."

**Metric:** Patreon conversion rate among repeat buyers (2+ pack purchases).

---

## Stage 6: Advocacy

**What they're thinking:** "I want to tell other producers about this."

**What they need:** Easy language and shareable assets. Producers don't advocate for things they can't explain quickly.

**What XO_OX provides:** Share-ready preview cards sized for social (square and vertical formats). A "made with XO_OX" tag that producers can use on beat posts. Community submissions program — producers share beats made with the packs, XO_OX reposts and credits. This creates a visible feedback loop that pulls in new Stage 1 traffic.

**What moves them forward:** Organic posts, Reddit comments naming the pack, collaborations. Each piece of word-of-mouth re-enters the funnel at Stage 1 or 2 with warmer trust than cold discovery.

**Metric:** Community-tagged posts per month (beats shared with "made with XO_OX" framing).

---

## Friction Points to Remove

**1. Discovery → sound must be beat-in-context.** A synth demo or preset sweep does not stop a scrolling producer. The Sound Post format is non-negotiable: groove first, preset name second.

**2. First Experience → MPCE_SETUP.md must be bundled.** Any instruction that requires leaving the file system to find documentation is a drop-off point. If it's not in the ZIP, it doesn't exist for a significant portion of buyers.

**3. Investment → price anchoring is structural.** TIDE TABLES being free is not marketing — it is the mechanism that makes $15 feel low-risk. Do not add friction to the free download or the anchor collapses.

**4. Commitment → Patreon day-one value.** If a new Patreon member joins and sees no immediate deliverable, they cancel in month two. The onboarding post and first exclusive asset must be present before the tier goes live.

**5. Advocacy → shareable assets must exist before asking for shares.** Preview cards, pack covers at social dimensions, and a consistent tag must be designed and available. Asking producers to advocate without giving them the materials is a dead end.
