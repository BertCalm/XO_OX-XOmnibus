# Community Challenge #3 — The Reef

**Launch date:** July 13, 2026 (Week 17)
**Results announced:** July 27 – August 2, 2026 (Week 19)
**Prize:** Winning preset added to the factory library (next release)

---

## The Challenge

Build a patch where the Aquatic FX Suite is a load-bearing sonic element — not decoration, but the actual character of the sound.

The Aquatic FX Suite has four stages: Reef (early reflections), Fathom (pressure compression), Drift (Brownian chorus), and Tide (tremolo / auto-filter). Most people treat these as final polish — a wash of space over a patch that already sounds complete. This challenge is about something different. We want to hear patches where the Aquatic FX chain is the reason the sound is interesting. Strip the FX and you strip the identity.

The challenge is named "The Reef" because a reef isn't a decoration on the ocean floor — it's an ecosystem. It structures everything that lives in it. The FX chain should function the same way: the engine lives inside the Aquatic FX the way coral lives inside a reef. The FX shapes the engine, the engine responds to the FX, and the result is a sound that neither could produce alone.

---

## Rules

**Engine count:** Any number of engines (1–4). Coupling is optional but permitted.

**FX requirement:** At least one Aquatic FX stage must be demonstrably active. "Demonstrably active" means: bypass the Aquatic FX on your patch and the sound is fundamentally different — not just quieter or less polished, but structurally different. A preset where the dry signal is interesting and the Aquatic FX is aesthetic additive space does not qualify.

**Submission format:** `.xometa` file + 2–5 sentences describing which Aquatic FX stage(s) drive the character of the sound and why you dialed them in the way you did.

**One submission per person.** You can update your submission until the deadline.

---

## What We're Judging

**FX integration.** Is the Aquatic FX chain essential or decorative? The test: remove it, and is the sound fundamentally altered? We're not asking for distortion or chaos — a very gentle Drift chorus at low mix can be essential if the sound depends on that movement. What matters is dependency, not intensity.

**Timbral originality.** Does your FX-as-instrument approach produce a sound that doesn't exist without this chain? Reef's early reflections as a physical resonator, Fathom's pressure compression as a spectral sculptor, Drift as the movement engine, Tide as the rhythmic pulse — we're listening for patches where the FX stage is doing synthesis work, not finishing work.

**FX choice intention.** Did you choose the right stage for the job, and did you set it with intention? Reef at size 0.9 and decay 0.8 is a design decision. Drift at 6 voices with a 0.2 Hz rate is a design decision. We're looking for parameters that were tuned, not defaults that happened to work.

**Playability.** Does the patch respond to velocity and touch in a way that the Aquatic FX makes interesting? A Tide-driven patch where note velocity changes the auto-filter frequency, or a Reef patch where harder playing pushes the early reflections into audible comb territory — that kind of interaction is what we're looking for.

🪸 **Community Challenge #3 — The Reef**

Use the Aquatic FX Suite as the instrument, not the finish.

The Aquatic FX chain in XOlokun — Reef, Fathom, Drift, Tide — is usually the last thing people touch. A bit of space, a little warmth, done. This challenge is about using those four stages as load-bearing structural elements: the reason your sound is what it is, not the gloss on top of it.

**Your challenge:**

Build a patch where bypassing the Aquatic FX fundamentally changes the sound — not just makes it drier, but makes it a different sound. The FX chain has to be doing synthesis work. Reef as a resonator. Fathom as a timbral sculptor. Drift as the movement engine. Tide as the rhythmic character. Pick one (or more) and build the patch around it.

**Rules:**
- Any engine(s), any coupling (or none)
- At least one Aquatic FX stage must be demonstrably active
- Bypassing the Aquatic FX must fundamentally change the sound, not just polish it
- Share your `.xometa` file + 2–5 sentences on which stage(s) drive the character and how you dialed them in

**Prize:** The winning patch gets added to the factory library in the next release, credited to you in the preset description.

This challenge runs for two weeks. Drop your entries in #challenge-the-reef.

Some starting points if you're stuck: Reef at size 0.85+ starts behaving like a physical resonator rather than a reverb — try pairing it with a dry, percussive engine and letting the Reef carry the sustain. Drift at 5–6 voices with low rate (0.01–0.05 Hz) is an entirely different thing from Drift as chorus — it's a slowly breathing spatial environment. Tide in auto-filter mode with a resonant sweep can completely define a patch's rhythmic character. The Fathom pressure compressor in deep mode with low crossover shapes low-end as texture, not control. The Field Guide post "The Three Chains" covers all four Aquatic stages with specific parameter recommendations (xo-ox.org/field-guide/guide-aquatic-fx.html).

## Judging Rubric (internal)

| Criterion | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| FX integration | 35% | Is the Aquatic FX chain load-bearing? Does bypassing it fundamentally change the sound, not just strip polish? |
| Timbral originality | 30% | Does this sound exist without this FX configuration? Is the FX doing synthesis work rather than finishing work? |
| FX choice intention | 20% | Were parameters set deliberately? Does the description explain the specific settings and why? Is this a design decision, not an accident? |
| Playability | 15% | Does the patch respond expressively to velocity and touch? Does player interaction create interesting relationships with the active FX stage(s)? |

Minimum score to win: 7/10 overall. If no submission reaches 7/10, announce honorable mentions and carry the prize to Challenge #4.
