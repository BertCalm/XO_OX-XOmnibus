The Origin

The Primordial Coupling

In the beginning there were two: feliX the neon tetra and Oscar the axolotl.

feliX is X — surface, transient, bright, rhythmic, electric. A flash of neon scales darting through sunlit shallows. He lives in the moment. Every note is an arrival. He is the snap of a tail changing direction, the crack of a wave breaking, the first millisecond of a sound before it knows what it will become.

Oscar is O — depth, sustain, warm, patient, regenerative. Pink gills breathing slowly in a deep cave. He lives in duration. Every note is a journey. He is the slow inhale of a tide, the geological patience of a reef growing one polyp at a time, the long tail of a sound after its origin is forgotten.

When feliX and Oscar coupled for the first time — X met O, transient met sustain, surface met depth — the XO_OX universe was born. Every engine that followed is a species that evolved from that primordial coupling. Every sound in XOlokun lives somewhere in the water column between feliX's sky and Oscar's floor.

The brand mark — XO_OX — is their names interleaved. X and O, forever coupled. The underscore is the surface of the water: everything above it belongs to feliX, everything below to Oscar, and the most alive sounds exist in both places at once.

feliX
The Neon Tetra — X
Surface, transient, bright, rhythmic, electric. A flash of iridescent blue darting through sunlit shallows. He is the initiator. He strikes first. Every note begins with a pitch sweep downward: the snap of a tail changing direction.
Oscar
The Axolotl — O
Depth, sustain, warm, patient, regenerative. Pink gills breathing slowly in a coral cave, regenerating, evolving, never rushing. He is the responder. He sustains. He absorbs the energy of other species and transforms it into warmth.

Coupling as Symbiosis

How Species Interact

In the XO_OX ecosystem, coupling is not a technical feature — it is how species interact. The most interesting sounds couple engines from different depths. A surface species driving a deep species creates vertical tension: the sound spans the entire water column.

AmpToFilter
Predator-Prey
One drives the other. The amplitude of the predator opens the filter of the prey — louder the strike, wider the jaws.
ONSET → OVERBITE: drum hit makes bass snarl
AudioToFM
Parasitic Modulation
One lives inside the other. Audio from the parasite modulates the carrier frequency of the host, creating timbres neither could produce alone.
OVERWORLD → OPAL: chip audio inside grain buffer
AudioToWavetable
Symbiotic Feeding
One nourishes the other. Audio output becomes the raw material for new synthesis — a living sound fed into a living process.
ODYSSEY → OPAL: pad output becomes grain source
EnvToMorph
Mimicry
One shapes itself to match the other. The envelope of the mimic drives the morph position of the model — becoming what it observes.
ODYSSEY → OCELOT: Climax bloom drives strata balance
getSample
Commensalism
One passes through the other. The commensal benefits from the environment without affecting it — sound colored by the space it travels through.
OSTINATO → OVERDUB: drum circle through dub FX
AmpToChoke
Territorial
One silences the other. Territorial exclusion — when one species sounds, the other retreats. Used sparingly. Most species coexist.
