Oscar v3 — Branching Gills

The gills are feathery branches, not sticks. Each one forks like a tiny tree.

Gill anatomy study (close-up): Real axolotl gills have a main ramus (stalk) with 2-3 fimbriae (feathery branches) on each side. The branches taper and curve outward. At small sizes we simplify to curved lines; at large sizes we show the full branching structure with translucent filament tips.

v2 → v3 changes: Gills are now <path> curves with Q-bezier bends instead of <line> sticks. Each gill has 2-3 branching filaments that taper in width and opacity. Filament tips have tiny glowing circles at large sizes. The sway animation applies to the entire gill group, so branches move as a unit (organic, not mechanical). Three opacity layers: main stalk (full), branches (.4-.6), filaments (.2-.35).
