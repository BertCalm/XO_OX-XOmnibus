In every synthesizer ever built, the control and the sound exist at the same position. When you move a knob, the sound jumps. XO_OX separates these: the setpoint ▲ is where you put the control; the value arc is where the DSP actually is, after smoothing. The distance between them is the performance gap — the physical feel of an acoustic instrument translated into synthesis. You are not a musician playing an instrument. You are a marine biologist managing an ecosystem. The creatures perform. You create the conditions.

The PlaySurface is not a keyboard. Each pad is a location in the ecological habitat. Your touch disturbs the environment at that location — and the creature responds from there. Velocity is disturbance intensity. The Fretless strip is the water column: slide down to dive deeper, move horizontally to change water temperature.

Design Anatomy — OpenBridge 6.1 + Apple CarPlay + XO_OX Ecology

Each instrument is composed of: Track ring (full range base) → Advice zone arcs (habitat/stress/critical, always visible at reduced opacity) → Value arc (live DSP output in engine accent color) → Setpoint marker (gold triangle, player intent) → Center face (engine creature icon + live value readout). The gap between setpoint and value arc is visible as the dashed gold line. This is the 9.8 interface.
