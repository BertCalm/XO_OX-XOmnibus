### What XOceanus Is

XOceanus is a multi-engine synthesizer that loads up to four engines simultaneously and routes modulation signals between them in real time. The coupling system — its defining feature — supports 15 distinct coupling types: gravitational, adversarial, topological, symbiotic, and others. When ONSET's percussive transients drive ORGANON's metabolic rate, the result is a sound that cannot be produced by either engine alone. This cross-engine relationship is the instrument's core proposition.

The engines span the full range of synthesis architecture: additive, subtractive, FM, granular, physical modeling, wavetable, cellular automata, and hybrid approaches. Each engine has its own parameter set, preset library, expression mapping, and identity within the instrument's aquatic mythology. Every engine responds to velocity, aftertouch, and mod wheel. Every engine has four macro controls and autonomous modulation.

The factory preset library ships with more than 19,500 presets organized across 15 mood categories and indexed by a six-dimensional Sonic DNA system covering brightness, warmth, movement, density, space, and aggression. Users can navigate by character rather than by preset number.

### About the Coupling System

The coupling system routes live audio and modulation signals between any loaded engines. The 15 coupling types produce structurally different relationships:

- **Gravitational** — amplitude of one engine bends the pitch of another
- **Adversarial** — engines compete; one suppresses the other based on amplitude
- **Symbiotic** — engines share energy; one feeds the other's synthesis parameters
- **Topological** — phase relationships are routed through mathematical knot matrices
- **Entrainment** — tempo and phase locking between engines over time

These relationships are not simple send/return effects. They are DSP-level signal exchanges that alter the fundamental character of both engines simultaneously.

### About the Mythology

XOceanus's engines are organized around an aquatic mythology. Each engine is a creature from a specific depth zone in the ocean — sunlit, twilight, or midnight — with its own evolutionary pressure, form, and behavior. The name XOceanus comes from Olokun, the Yoruba orisha of the unfathomable deep. "XOceanus — for all" is the product's motto.

The mythology is not decorative. The depth placement determines how engines are grouped in the aquarium browser. The creature identity informs the preset character and macro naming. The Field Guide — 15 articles and more than 52,000 words — documents the sonic ecology in depth.
