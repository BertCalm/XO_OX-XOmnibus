Manifesto

We build instruments that sound like something.

Sound has character.

Not features. Not specifications. Not oscillator counts or filter slopes or modulation depth. Character — the unnamed quality that makes you reach for one instrument and ignore twenty others. The thing that makes a sound yours before you've touched a single knob.

We build for that. Everything else is in service of it.

The raw sound must sing.

Before reverb. Before delay. Before saturation. If the dry oscillator doesn't make you lean forward, no amount of processing will save it. Effects enhance character. They never create it.

When you initialize an XO_OX preset, the first thing you hear should be compelling. Not a blank canvas. Not a starting point. A statement.

Presets are the product.

Not afterthoughts. Not demo filler. Not the thing you scroll past on the way to init. The factory presets are the first experience a musician has with an instrument — and for most, the only experience. They must demonstrate the instrument's reason to exist.

Over twenty-four hundred presets ship with XOmnibus. Every one was shaped to make you hear something the engine was born to do. Not filler. Not padding. A library that rewards browsing.

Instruments should talk to each other.

A synth alone is a voice. Two synths listening to each other are a conversation. Twenty-six synths modulating each other in real time are an ecosystem — unpredictable, alive, capable of sounds no single instrument could produce.

Coupling is the difference between a rack of instruments and a living system. We chose the living system.

It started with two creatures.

feliX the neon tetra — bright, darting, electric. The snap of a transient. The flash of a note attack. The X.

Oscar the axolotl — patient, morphing, regenerative. The sustain of a held chord. The slow bloom of a pad. The O.

Every engine in XOmnibus carries DNA from both. The interplay between snap and sustain, between surface and depth, between the bright dart and the slow bloom — that tension is the source of character. Not one or the other. Both.

They live in the same water. That water is the Aquarium — six master effects named for ocean phenomena, the sonic environment where every engine's voice passes through on its way to your speakers. feliX near the surface. Oscar in the depths. The music happens in between.

Sound belongs to everyone.

Every XO_OX instrument is free. Open source. No subscriptions. No dongles. No email gates. No "free tier" with a ceiling you'll hit next Tuesday. No artificial scarcity for something that costs nothing to copy.

This is not a business model. This is a belief. If you make something that matters, you give it to the people who need it. The music comes first. Always.

What we reject.

  • Feature lists as identity. More oscillators do not make a better instrument.
  • Spec sheet worship. No one ever cried because of a filter slope.
  • Blank-canvas design. An init patch is a failure of imagination.
  • The preset tax. Factory sounds are not a teaser for a paid expansion.
  • Artificial scarcity. Digital goods that cost nothing to copy should cost nothing to own.
  • The loudness arms race. Louder is not better. Louder is just louder.
  • Swiss-army-knife synthesis. An instrument that does everything sounds like nothing.

One person. One AI. No limits.

XO_OX Designs is one musician building synthesizers in partnership with AI. Not a company. Not a startup. Not a brand looking for market fit. A person who wanted instruments with character and couldn't find enough of them in the world — and found a collaborator that removed every technical ceiling between imagination and implementation.

Twenty-six engines. Sixteen hundred presets. Tens of thousands of lines of DSP code. A brand site, a field guide, an aquatic mythology, an entire sonic universe — built by one creative vision amplified by AI. The ideas, the taste, the decisions about what sounds right and what doesn't — those are human. The ability to execute at this scale without compromise — that's the partnership.

This is what happens when you remove the bottleneck between what a musician can imagine and what they can build. Not AI replacing creativity. AI enabling it.

We make instruments that sound like themselves.
We give them away because we mean it.
We build without limits because we found a way.
We build character because the world has enough features.
