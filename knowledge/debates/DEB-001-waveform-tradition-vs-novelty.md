# DEB-001: Waveform Convention vs. Spectral Novelty

**Ghosts**: Buchla (novelty) vs. Moog (trust the filter)
**Engine**: OBRIX (2026-03-19)
**Status**: Unresolved — productive tension

**Buchla's position**: Five of seven standard waveforms (Saw, Square, Triangle, Pulse, Noise) are test-equipment artifacts from the 1960s. They became the vocabulary of synthesis because they were cheap to generate, not because they are the right vocabulary. A synthesis engine in 2026 should reach toward spectral content that couldn't exist before — complex oscillators, waveshaping networks, ocean-sourced wavetables.

**Moog's position**: The SVF filter is genuine engineering. Trust the filter to transform conventional waveforms into something expressive. The waveform is the raw material; the filter is the instrument.

**Resolution**: Both are right — conventional waveforms with excellent filter design is one dimension; novel spectral content (ocean-sourced wavetables, complex oscillator FM coupling) is another. OBRIX should pursue both tracks simultaneously: keep the conventional waveforms AND build a distinctly oceanic wavetable bank that makes Buchla proud.
